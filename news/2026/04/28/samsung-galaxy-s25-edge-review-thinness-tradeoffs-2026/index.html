Samsung Galaxy S25 Edge Review: Extreme Thinness Comes With Tradeoffs

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-28

⚡ Quick Summary

  • The Samsung Galaxy S25 Edge pushes smartphone thinness to new extremes but sacrifices battery life and thermal management.
  • At just 5.8mm thick, it is among the thinnest flagship phones ever made, with premium materials and striking design.
  • Battery life under heavy use falls short of thicker competitors, and sustained performance throttles under thermal load.
  • The S25 Edge is a statement piece for users who prioritise aesthetics and portability above raw endurance.

Samsung's Galaxy S25 Edge is a fascinating device — not because it is the best smartphone on the market, but because it represents a design philosophy pushed to its logical extreme and the tradeoffs that result. At 5.8mm thick, it is one of the thinnest flagship phones ever manufactured, and the engineering required to achieve that thinness while maintaining flagship-level performance is impressive. But the compromises are real, and they matter for enterprise users evaluating device fleets.

The Thinness Achievement

5.8mm is genuinely thin. To put it in context, the iPhone 16 is 7.8mm, the standard Galaxy S25 is 7.2mm, and the Galaxy S25 Ultra is 8.2mm. The S25 Edge shaves 1.4mm off the standard S25 — which does not sound like much until you hold both phones side by side. The Edge feels like a completely different category of device: more like a high-end credit card than a traditional smartphone.

The design execution is excellent. Samsung has used premium materials throughout — titanium frame, Gorilla Glass Victus 3, and a flat display that integrates seamlessly with the body. The camera module is remarkably shallow, maintaining the thin profile even on the back. The device is light at 162g, which combined with the thinness makes it exceptionally comfortable for one-handed use and pocket carrying.

The Battery Tradeoff

Physics is unforgiving: a thinner phone means less space for battery cells. The S25 Edge packs a 3,700mAh battery — significantly below the 4,000mAh+ capacities in competing flagships. Samsung's power management and the Snapdragon 8 Elite's efficiency help, but the fundamental constraint is real.

In practical testing, moderate use — email, messaging, web browsing, occasional photos — gets through a full day with 15-20% remaining. Heavy use — sustained video calls, navigation, photography, and app switching — drains the battery in 5-7 hours of screen time. That is below the 8-10 hour benchmark for premium phones and means that enterprise users who rely on their phone for a full business day without charging access should consider alternatives.

Thermal Management

The thin body also constrains thermal dissipation. Under sustained load — gaming, video rendering, or extended camera use — the S25 Edge throttles performance more aggressively than thicker counterparts. The Snapdragon 8 Elite is a powerful chip, but it cannot sustain peak performance when the thermal solution is a slim vapor chamber with limited surface area for heat dissipation.

For most everyday tasks, this is not noticeable. But for users who run demanding applications or need sustained peak performance, the throttling is real and measurable. Benchmark scores drop 15-20% under sustained load compared to the same chip in the thicker S25 Ultra chassis.

The Verdict for Enterprise Users

The Galaxy S25 Edge is a device for a specific user profile: someone who values design, portability, and the premium feel of an ultra-thin phone above all else. If that is your priority — and for many executives and client-facing professionals, it genuinely is — the S25 Edge delivers an unmatched experience.

For fleet deployments and users who need reliable all-day battery life under heavy use, the standard Galaxy S25 or S25 Ultra are better choices. The Edge is a statement device, not a workhorse — and as long as that distinction is clear, it is an excellent piece of engineering that pushes the boundaries of smartphone design in a direction that will appeal to a meaningful segment of the market.

Frequently Asked Questions

How thin is the Galaxy S25 Edge?

At 5.8mm, it is one of the thinnest flagship smartphones ever produced. For comparison, the iPhone 16 is 7.8mm and the standard Galaxy S25 is 7.2mm.

Is the battery life really that bad?

Under moderate use it lasts a full day. Under heavy use — gaming, video streaming, navigation — expect 5-7 hours of screen time, which is below the 8-10 hour benchmark for flagship phones.

Who should buy the S25 Edge?

Users who prioritise design, portability, and the feel of an ultra-thin device. Power users and heavy gamers should look at the Galaxy S25 Ultra or equivalent.
