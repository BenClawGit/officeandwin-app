Apple Unveils Liquid Glass: The Biggest Visual Redesign in a Decade

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-28

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Apple has announced Liquid Glass, a sweeping new design language unifying iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, and visionOS.
  • The system replaces flat frosted-glass aesthetics with dynamic translucency responding to content, light, and motion.
  • Liquid Glass is the most significant visual refresh since iOS 7 in 2013.
  • Developers will need to audit custom UI elements for compatibility.

Apple has unveiled Liquid Glass, a new software design language that will reshape the visual identity of every platform the company ships — iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, and visionOS — in a single coordinated update. This is the most consequential visual change to Apple's operating systems since iOS 7 replaced skeuomorphic design with flat aesthetics in 2013.

What Liquid Glass Actually Is

Liquid Glass is a rendering approach that treats UI surfaces as dynamically translucent materials responding to the content beneath them, ambient lighting, and user motion. Unlike the static frosted-glass effects that have defined Apple's platforms for years, Liquid Glass surfaces shift in real time: a sheet pulled up over a photograph picks up hues from the image; a notification banner appearing over video modulates its opacity based on content brightness; a control centre panel responds to viewing angle, simulating depth and material thickness.

Apple designers describe it as material honesty — surfaces that acknowledge they are glass and light rather than paper or leather, but behave in ways that feel grounded and tactile. The design draws heavily from the visual language Apple developed for visionOS and brings it to flat-screen devices for the first time.

Why Now

The timing reflects the maturation of Apple's spatial computing strategy. Apple Vision Pro introduced a UI paradigm built around physical space, depth, and perceived volume. The existing flat design language of iOS and macOS looked increasingly incongruous against that backdrop. Liquid Glass provides a visual bridge: depth and material responsiveness on flat screens, ensuring that interfaces appearing in spatial contexts on Vision Pro feel native rather than ported.

The competitive context matters too. Android 16 and Google's Material You have continued evolving in expressive directions. Samsung's One UI pushes dynamic theming aggressively. Apple's flat design, largely unchanged in its fundamentals since 2013, had begun to look static by comparison. Liquid Glass is a strong response demonstrating that Apple still views design differentiation as a core strategic asset.

Platform Coverage

Liquid Glass ships across all five Apple platforms simultaneously — itself notable, since previous design updates typically landed on iOS first and propagated to macOS and watchOS over subsequent cycles. The synchronised rollout signals that Apple views design coherence across the full product line as a higher priority than the traditional mobile-first sequence.

On macOS, the changes affect the menu bar, dock, system panels, and notification centre. On iOS and iPadOS, the home screen, control centre, lock screen widgets, and all system sheets are updated. On watchOS, complications and the notification stack get the treatment. On visionOS, Liquid Glass replaces the existing window chrome system with a more expressive material stack leveraging the headset's spatial rendering capabilities.

What Developers Need to Do

For most developers building with standard UIKit, SwiftUI, or AppKit components, the transition will be largely automatic. Apple's frameworks apply Liquid Glass rendering to system-provided elements without requiring code changes. The challenge is custom UI — components with developer-drawn rendering, opaque backgrounds, or hard-coded colours that assumed the previous aesthetic.

Apple is providing an audit checklist in the developer documentation and updated Xcode tooling to flag compatibility issues at build time. Developers in categories where visual polish is commercially significant — productivity, media, health, finance — should prioritise testing immediately after beta access opens. The broader opportunity is creative: Liquid Glass introduces new APIs for custom material rendering and depth-aware layouts that enable interface experiences that genuinely could not have been built under the previous design system.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will Liquid Glass reach users?

Apple is expected to roll out Liquid Glass with iOS 20 and macOS 16 later in 2026. Developer betas should arrive shortly after WWDC.

Does Liquid Glass affect app performance?

Apple engineered Liquid Glass to run efficiently on A-series and M-series chips. Older devices may see reduced effect fidelity to maintain performance.

What do developers need to change?

Apps using standard UIKit, SwiftUI, or AppKit components adapt automatically. Custom UI with hard-coded opaque backgrounds will require explicit updates.
