OpenAI Partners With Jony Ive's io Startup to Build AI Hardware

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-28

⚡ Quick Summary

  • OpenAI has signed a deal with Jony Ive's startup io to develop AI hardware products designed by the legendary Apple designer.
  • The partnership signals OpenAI's ambition to move beyond software into physical AI devices.
  • Ive's design philosophy could differentiate OpenAI hardware in a market currently dominated by smart speakers and phones.
  • The first product is rumoured to be a screenless, voice-first personal AI device.

OpenAI has announced a partnership with Jony Ive's design firm io to develop AI hardware products, marking the ChatGPT maker's most significant move beyond pure software into the physical device market. The deal pairs the world's most prominent AI company with the designer responsible for the most successful consumer electronics of the 21st century — and it signals that the AI hardware race is about to get substantially more interesting.

Why Hardware, Why Now

OpenAI's core business is software: large language models, APIs, and the ChatGPT application. But the company's leadership has repeatedly signalled that the future of AI interaction will not be confined to text boxes on screens. Sam Altman has spoken publicly about AI needing to be ambient — available wherever you are, without requiring you to pull out a phone and type a prompt. Hardware is the obvious path to that vision.

The competitive dynamics reinforce the logic. Apple is embedding AI directly into iPhones and Macs through Apple Intelligence, giving it a hardware surface that OpenAI cannot access. Google controls Android and Pixel. Amazon has Echo. Meta has Quest. Each major AI competitor owns a hardware platform that delivers its AI to users in a default, frictionless way. OpenAI, despite having the most recognised AI brand, has no hardware surface of its own. The io partnership is designed to change that.

What Jony Ive Brings

Jony Ive's design legacy at Apple is unmatched: the iMac, iPod, iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch, and Apple Vision Pro all bear his design DNA. After leaving Apple in 2019, he founded io as a design collective working on selective, high-impact projects. The firm has maintained a deliberately low profile, taking on only projects where Ive's involvement would meaningfully shape the outcome.

What Ive brings to an AI hardware project is not just aesthetic sensibility — though that matters enormously — but a philosophy of product design that starts from human interaction rather than technical capability. The iPhone was not the first smartphone; it was the first smartphone designed around the assumption that a fingertip was the best input method. Similarly, an Ive-designed AI device is unlikely to be a better screen-based assistant; it will be designed around a thesis about how humans should interact with ambient AI.

The Screenless Thesis

Industry sources and reporting suggest the first OpenAI-io product will be a screenless, voice-first device — a form factor that several startups have attempted but none has yet made mainstream. The logic is sound: if AI's value is in understanding natural language and generating intelligent responses, a screen is often unnecessary overhead. A device you talk to, that talks back, and that handles complex tasks through voice — scheduling, research, communication, home control — without requiring visual interaction could be genuinely transformative for accessibility, multitasking, and ambient computing.

The challenge, of course, is that Humane's AI Pin and Rabbit's R1 both attempted similar visions and both failed commercially. The difference Ive and OpenAI bring is design authority and AI capability respectively. Whether that combination overcomes the fundamental UX challenges of screenless computing — how to handle ambiguity, how to provide feedback without visual confirmation, how to manage complex multi-step tasks through voice alone — remains the central question.

For the Enterprise

The immediate enterprise relevance is limited — this is a consumer device play. But the longer-term implication is significant. If OpenAI successfully creates a new hardware category for AI interaction, it establishes a direct channel to users that bypasses Apple, Google, and Microsoft platforms entirely. For enterprises building on OpenAI's API, that means the future user base for AI-powered products may increasingly live on OpenAI-controlled hardware, creating new integration opportunities and dependencies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Jony Ive's startup io?

io is Jony Ive's design firm founded after he left Apple in 2019. It focuses on design projects across technology, and Ive has been selective about partnerships. The OpenAI deal is one of the firm's highest-profile collaborations.

What kind of hardware will OpenAI and io build?

Details are unconfirmed, but industry sources suggest a screenless, voice-first personal AI device — a dedicated hardware form factor for interacting with AI assistants without a phone or computer.

When will the first product ship?

No timeline has been announced. Given Ive's meticulous design process and the complexity of new hardware categories, a realistic expectation is late 2027 or 2028.
