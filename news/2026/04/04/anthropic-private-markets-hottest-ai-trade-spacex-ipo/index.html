Anthropic Dominates Private Markets as Hottest AI Trade While SpaceX IPO Looms Large

What Happened

Anthropic has emerged as the single hottest trade in the private secondary market, according to Glen Anderson, president of Rainmaker Securities, a firm specializing in pre-IPO share transactions. In a detailed analysis reported by TechCrunch, Anderson describes the secondary market for private technology shares as never more active — with Anthropic commanding unprecedented demand from investors eager to secure positions before an eventual public listing.

The surge in Anthropic's private market valuation comes as rival OpenAI appears to be losing ground among secondary market traders. While OpenAI remains enormously valuable, the shift in investor preference toward Anthropic reflects growing confidence in the company's technology leadership, business model sustainability, and the strength of its enterprise customer relationships. Claude, Anthropic's flagship AI assistant, has gained significant market share in both consumer and enterprise segments throughout 2025 and early 2026.

However, the entire private technology market faces a potential disruption: SpaceX's anticipated IPO could reshape the landscape by releasing billions in liquidity that is currently locked in SpaceX shares. When SpaceX goes public, the flood of capital freed from one of the most widely held private companies could either boost demand for other private investments like Anthropic — or divert attention entirely to the public markets, reducing private market activity.

Background and Context

The secondary market for private technology company shares has grown dramatically over the past decade, evolving from an informal, relationship-driven space to a more structured marketplace with dedicated platforms and brokers. Companies like Rainmaker Securities, Forge Global, and EquityZen facilitate transactions between current shareholders (often employees or early investors) and buyers seeking pre-IPO exposure to high-growth technology companies.

Anthropic's journey from a research-focused startup to the hottest private market trade has been remarkably rapid. Founded in 2021 by former OpenAI executives Dario and Daniela Amodei, the company has raised billions in funding from investors including Google, Salesforce, and various venture capital firms. Its valuation has climbed from approximately $4 billion in early 2023 to estimates exceeding $60 billion in recent secondary market transactions — a trajectory that reflects both the company's technological achievements and the broader AI investment frenzy.

OpenAI's relative decline in secondary market popularity is notable. While the company's ChatGPT remains the most widely recognized AI consumer product, concerns about corporate governance, the transition from nonprofit to for-profit structure, and questions about competitive positioning have dampened some investor enthusiasm. The secondary market, which tends to reflect forward-looking sentiment rather than current performance, appears to be pricing in a view that Anthropic's trajectory is steeper than OpenAI's.

Why This Matters

The private market dynamics around AI companies provide a valuable signal about institutional investor sentiment — one that often predicts public market behavior. The strong demand for Anthropic shares suggests that sophisticated investors believe the company is well-positioned for significant appreciation when it eventually goes public. This confidence is built on several factors: Claude's improving benchmark performance, strong enterprise adoption, a safety-focused approach that appeals to regulated industries, and a leadership team with deep technical credibility.

The contrast with OpenAI is particularly significant. For years, OpenAI was synonymous with the AI revolution — its ChatGPT launch in November 2022 is widely credited with igniting the current AI boom. The shift in investor preference toward Anthropic suggests that the market is beginning to differentiate between companies based on execution, technology, and business fundamentals rather than simply riding the AI hype wave. This maturation of investor assessment is healthy for the industry, even if it creates winners and losers.

SpaceX's potential IPO adds an unpredictable variable. As one of the most widely held positions in the private market, SpaceX going public would release substantial capital and also demonstrate the public market's appetite for premium technology offerings. A successful SpaceX IPO could set a positive precedent for Anthropic and other AI companies considering their own public listings, while a disappointing debut could cool the entire private technology market. The interconnection between the space and AI sectors in investor portfolios is a unique feature of the current market cycle.

Industry Impact

Anthropic's private market dominance has practical implications for the AI industry's competitive dynamics. Strong investor support translates into greater access to capital, which funds research, infrastructure, and talent acquisition. Anthropic's ability to raise money at favorable valuations gives it a meaningful competitive advantage in the capital-intensive AI race, where building and operating large language models requires billions in compute infrastructure investment.

The secondary market activity also affects AI talent acquisition. Engineers and researchers evaluating job offers from competing AI companies factor in equity compensation and liquidity expectations. Anthropic's strong secondary market — which suggests meaningful appreciation potential and likely public listing — makes its equity compensation more attractive, potentially drawing talent from competitors where equity is less liquid or valuations are perceived as closer to their ceiling.

For enterprise customers evaluating AI vendors, the financial health and investor confidence signals from private markets provide useful input for vendor selection decisions. Organizations investing in AI integration — building workflows around specific providers' APIs and models — want confidence that their chosen vendor will be well-funded and operational for years to come. Companies building their AI strategies alongside enterprise productivity software like Microsoft's Copilot ecosystem are making long-term bets that benefit from provider stability.

The broader venture capital ecosystem is also affected. Limited partners evaluating AI-focused venture funds look at secondary market performance as a leading indicator of eventual returns. Anthropic's strong showing validates the thesis of funds that invested early, potentially freeing additional capital for the next generation of AI startups.

Expert Perspective

Private market analysts caution that secondary market valuations can be volatile and don't always translate directly to public market performance. The premium that private market buyers are willing to pay for Anthropic shares reflects a combination of genuine business optimism and FOMO-driven demand that could moderate as the IPO window approaches. History shows that some of the most anticipated private market trades — including several high-profile technology IPOs — have underperformed initial expectations.

However, the fundamentals supporting Anthropic's valuation are stronger than many previous private market darlings. The AI market is growing rapidly, Anthropic's technology is competitive with or ahead of alternatives in many benchmarks, and the company's safety-focused positioning creates a differentiated brand that appeals to enterprise buyers and regulators. These factors suggest that Anthropic's private market premium, while aggressive, is supported by real competitive advantages.

The SpaceX variable introduces genuine uncertainty. Anderson of Rainmaker Securities has noted that the sheer volume of capital currently allocated to SpaceX in private portfolios means that its IPO could create significant rebalancing activity. Whether that rebalancing benefits or hurts other private market names like Anthropic depends on market conditions and investor sentiment at the time of the listing.

What This Means for Businesses

Organizations evaluating AI strategy should note the market's growing confidence in Anthropic as a signal about the competitive landscape. Companies that have been evaluating Claude for enterprise deployment may find the financial stability signal from private market activity reassuring — a well-funded provider with strong investor backing is more likely to maintain investment in product development, customer support, and infrastructure. Pairing AI tools with current affordable Microsoft Office licence deployments ensures organizations have both the AI capabilities and the foundational productivity tools needed for modern workflows.

For technology investors and employees of AI companies, the secondary market dynamics provide useful intelligence about relative positioning. The shift from OpenAI to Anthropic as the preferred private market trade reflects a meaningful change in consensus that should inform both investment and career decisions. Employees with equity in either company should consult financial advisors about liquidity options in the current market environment.

Business leaders should also track the SpaceX IPO timeline as a barometer for the broader technology market. A successful SpaceX public offering would likely create positive momentum for subsequent AI company IPOs, potentially including Anthropic. This timeline matters for organizations planning genuine Windows 11 key and broader technology infrastructure investments that may benefit from increased vendor competition and innovation driven by well-funded AI competitors.

Looking Ahead

The timeline for Anthropic's own public listing remains uncertain, but private market activity suggests that the company is approaching the scale and maturity where an IPO becomes a natural next step. The SpaceX IPO, whenever it occurs, will provide the most significant test of public market appetite for premium technology offerings since the AI boom began. Both events will have cascading effects on AI investment, competition, and the pace of innovation across the industry. For now, the private market's verdict is clear: Anthropic is the AI company that investors most want to own heading into the second half of 2026.
