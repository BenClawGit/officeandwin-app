New Xbox CEO Admits Game Pass Has Become Too Expensive for Players

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-13

⚡ Quick Summary

  • New Xbox CEO Asha Sharma has acknowledged internally that Game Pass has become too expensive for players, according to a leaked internal memo
  • Microsoft implemented major Game Pass price hikes in 2025 that proved deeply unpopular with the gaming community
  • Sharma says Microsoft needs to find a better value equation for subscribers while maintaining the service's content quality
  • The admission signals potential pricing changes ahead for Microsoft's gaming subscription service

Microsoft's new Xbox CEO Asha Sharma has acknowledged that the Xbox Game Pass subscription service has become too expensive for players, according to an internal memo obtained by The Verge. The admission is remarkable both for its candor and its timing — Sharma took over as Xbox CEO only recently, and publicly criticizing a core product's pricing strategy is virtually unheard of in the technology industry.

The memo reportedly states that Microsoft needs a better value equation for Game Pass subscribers, suggesting that the company is aware that its 2025 price increases pushed the service beyond what many players consider reasonable. The price hikes affected all Game Pass tiers, with the top-tier Game Pass Ultimate rising to $19.99 per month in most markets — a significant increase that drew widespread criticism from the gaming community.

Game Pass has been central to Microsoft's gaming strategy since its launch in 2017. The service offers access to hundreds of games for a monthly fee, positioned as the Netflix of gaming. However, unlike streaming video services where content costs are relatively predictable, game development budgets have ballooned dramatically — particularly for the AAA titles that Game Pass needs to attract and retain subscribers. Microsoft's acquisition of Activision Blizzard for $69 billion was largely justified as a way to populate Game Pass with must-have content, but the cost of that acquisition creates pressure to monetize subscribers more aggressively.

The pricing tension reflects a broader challenge facing subscription services across the technology industry. Microsoft 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, and dozens of other software-as-a-service products have all implemented above-inflation price increases in recent years, testing the limits of what subscribers will tolerate. When the value proposition — what you get for what you pay — tips too far toward the cost side, subscribers churn.

For Microsoft, Game Pass pricing also affects the broader Xbox ecosystem. Players who cancel Game Pass may be less likely to purchase games through the Xbox Store, reducing Microsoft's cut of third-party game sales. They may also be less likely to remain in the Xbox ecosystem at all, migrating to PC gaming platforms or competing consoles. The cost of losing a Game Pass subscriber extends well beyond the subscription revenue itself.

Sharma's memo does not outline specific pricing changes, but the acknowledgment of the problem suggests that adjustments are being considered. Whether Microsoft can reduce prices without compromising content quality or profitability remains to be seen. The gaming community will be watching closely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Xbox Game Pass cost now?

Game Pass Ultimate costs $19.99 per month. The PC and console tiers vary by region. Prices were increased significantly in 2025, drawing widespread criticism.

Who is the new Xbox CEO?

Asha Sharma became Xbox CEO in 2026. She previously held leadership roles at Microsoft and has acknowledged that Game Pass pricing needs adjustment.

Will Game Pass prices come down?

The internal memo suggests Microsoft is considering changes to improve the value equation. No specific price reductions have been announced, but the acknowledgment signals potential adjustments.

How many Game Pass subscribers are there?

Microsoft last reported over 34 million Game Pass subscribers. Growth has slowed as the service saturates its core market and pricing concerns discourage new sign-ups.

Does Game Pass include all Xbox games?

No. Game Pass includes a rotating library of hundreds of games, but many titles — especially new releases from non-Microsoft publishers — are not included and must be purchased separately.

How does Game Pass pricing compare to other subscriptions?

At $19.99/month for Ultimate, Game Pass is more expensive than most streaming video services but cheaper than buying games individually. The value depends on how many games you play monthly.
