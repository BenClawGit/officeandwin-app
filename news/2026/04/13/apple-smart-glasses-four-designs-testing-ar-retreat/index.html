Apple Testing Four Smart Glasses Designs as It Retreats From Ambitious AR Vision

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-13

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Apple is reportedly testing four different designs for upcoming smart glasses, signaling a more pragmatic approach than its ambitious original AR plans
  • The project represents a step back from earlier visions of a full range of mixed and augmented reality devices
  • Smart glasses would compete directly with Meta's Ray-Ban collaboration and Amazon's Echo Frames in the emerging wearable AI category
  • The move suggests Apple is prioritizing a shippable product over a revolutionary one after the mixed reception of Vision Pro

Apple is testing four different designs for a smart glasses product, according to people familiar with the matter, marking a significant step back from the ambitious augmented reality vision that once called for a full lineup of AR and mixed reality devices. The testing phase suggests Apple is taking a more pragmatic approach — prioritizing a product it can actually ship over a revolutionary concept that may take years to perfect.

The four designs reportedly range from lightweight glasses-style frames with integrated displays to more conventional eyewear that relies primarily on audio and camera-based AI features. The variation in designs reflects an internal debate at Apple about what a first-generation smart glasses product should be: a full-fledged AR display, an AI-powered camera glasses device, or something in between.

Meta has set the competitive benchmark with its Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses, which have found surprising commercial success by focusing on a simple value proposition: take photos, record video, listen to music, and interact with Meta AI — all from eyewear that looks like normal sunglasses. The Ray-Ban Meta glasses do not attempt to project augmented reality overlays into the wearer's field of view, which eliminates the technical challenges of transparent displays, eye tracking, and spatial computing that have made Vision Pro so expensive and heavy.

Apple's Vision Pro, launched at $3,499 in 2024, demonstrated that the company could build a technically impressive spatial computing device but struggled to find a mass market. Sales have been modest, and developer interest has waned as the installed base remains small compared to other Apple platforms. The smart glasses initiative appears to be an acknowledgment that the path to wearable computing at scale runs through lightweight, everyday-wearable devices rather than premium headsets.

For enterprise users, the smart glasses category has potential in field service, logistics, and manufacturing use cases where hands-free access to information, communication, and AI assistance can improve productivity. Microsoft's HoloLens explored this territory but remained a niche product at its premium price point. A lighter, more affordable approach from Apple — or Meta — could expand the enterprise market significantly.

Timeline expectations should be tempered. Apple's hardware development cycles for entirely new product categories typically span 3-5 years from early prototyping to commercial availability. Even if Apple selects a design this year, a consumer product is unlikely before 2027. The company is known for entering categories late but executing well, as it did with the Apple Watch, AirPods, and iPhone.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will Apple release smart glasses?

No official timeline exists. Apple is in the testing phase with four designs. Based on Apple's typical development cycles, a consumer product is unlikely before 2027.

How will Apple smart glasses compare to Vision Pro?

They will be much lighter, cheaper, and more focused on everyday use. Vision Pro remains a premium spatial computing device; smart glasses target the mass market.

What features would Apple smart glasses have?

Likely a combination of camera, speakers, microphone, and AI assistant capabilities. Some designs may include small integrated displays for notifications and simple information.

How do Meta Ray-Ban glasses compare?

Meta Ray-Ban glasses focus on photos, video, music, and AI chat without AR displays. They have been commercially successful, setting a benchmark for the category.

Will smart glasses replace Vision Pro?

No. They serve different use cases. Vision Pro is for immersive spatial computing; smart glasses are for lightweight, all-day wearable AI assistance and communication.

Could Apple smart glasses work with Microsoft 365?

Likely. If Apple smart glasses include AI assistant capabilities, integration with productivity services including Microsoft 365 would be expected through standard APIs.
