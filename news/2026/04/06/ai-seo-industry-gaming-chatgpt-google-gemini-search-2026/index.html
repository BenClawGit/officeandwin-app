The SEO Industry Is Racing to Game AI Search Results and It Might Actually Work

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-04-06

⚡ Quick Summary

  • SEO agencies are actively selling AI optimisation services to influence chatbot recommendations
  • Techniques aim to increase brand mentions in Google AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Gemini responses
  • AI responses carry more authority than traditional search results but lack commercial disclosure
  • Businesses should focus on authentic brand presence as the most sustainable AI visibility strategy

What Happened

A new front has opened in the search engine optimisation wars, and this time the battlefield is artificial intelligence. According to an in-depth investigation by The Verge, the SEO industry is aggressively experimenting with techniques to influence how AI systems like Google's AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Gemini reference and recommend brands in their responses. The practice, which some are calling "AI SEO" or "generative engine optimisation," represents a fundamental shift in how businesses think about online visibility.

The investigation reveals that marketing agencies and consultants are already selling AI optimisation services to enterprise clients, promising to increase the likelihood that AI chatbots mention their products and services when users ask relevant questions. Techniques range from the mundane — ensuring brand information is widely and consistently represented across authoritative sources that AI models train on — to the more aggressive, including structured data manipulation and strategic content placement designed to exploit known patterns in how large language models retrieve and synthesise information.

Perhaps most striking is the confidence some practitioners express about their ability to influence AI outputs. Multiple agencies reported measurable increases in AI brand mentions for their clients, though the methodologies for tracking these changes remain nascent and largely proprietary. The field is moving faster than the measurement tools needed to evaluate it.

Background and Context

Traditional SEO has spent two decades evolving alongside Google's search algorithm, developing from crude keyword stuffing into a sophisticated discipline encompassing technical optimisation, content strategy, link building, and user experience design. Each major Google algorithm update — Panda, Penguin, Hummingbird, BERT — forced the industry to adapt, creating an ongoing cat-and-mouse dynamic between search engines and optimisers.

The emergence of AI-powered search represents the most significant disruption to this dynamic since Google's founding. When a user asks ChatGPT or Google's AI Mode for a product recommendation, the AI does not return a ranked list of links — it provides a synthesised answer that may mention specific brands, often with reasoning for why those brands are recommended. This fundamentally changes the optimisation target from "rank highly in a list" to "be mentioned favourably in a conversation."

The shift is particularly consequential because AI responses tend to carry more implied authority than traditional search results. When Google returns ten blue links, users understand they are seeing suggestions to evaluate. When an AI chatbot recommends a specific product by name, users are more likely to interpret that as a considered endorsement. The perceived authority gap makes AI placement potentially more valuable — and more consequential — than traditional search ranking.

Why This Matters

The rise of AI SEO raises profound questions about the integrity of AI-generated information. If marketing agencies can systematically influence what AI systems recommend, then AI responses become another advertising channel rather than an objective information source. Users who turn to AI chatbots specifically because they want unbiased recommendations may find themselves receiving commercially influenced answers without any disclosure.

Unlike traditional search results, where paid placements are clearly labelled as advertisements, AI responses currently provide no indication of whether a brand mention is organic or influenced by optimisation efforts. This transparency gap is likely to become a regulatory concern as AI search adoption grows. Consumers have a right to know when the information they receive has been shaped by commercial interests, and the current generation of AI search products provides no mechanism for this disclosure.

For businesses, the emergence of AI SEO creates a new competitive dimension that may advantage well-resourced companies capable of investing in cutting-edge optimisation techniques. Small and medium businesses that struggle to keep up with traditional SEO may find themselves even further disadvantaged in AI search, where the rules are less understood, the tools are less accessible, and the feedback loops are slower and harder to measure.

Industry Impact

The SEO industry is experiencing a gold rush mentality around AI optimisation, with agencies racing to establish expertise and capture early-mover advantage. Conference presentations, training courses, and proprietary tools focused on AI SEO have proliferated rapidly, though the field's youth means much of the advice remains speculative rather than empirically validated.

Google faces a particularly delicate challenge. The company's AI Mode is designed to provide helpful, accurate answers to user queries, but if SEO practitioners can systematically game these responses, it undermines the product's core value proposition. Google has spent decades combating manipulation of its traditional search results and now faces the same challenge with a fundamentally different technology stack.

For AI model developers more broadly — OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, and others — the ability of external actors to influence model outputs through strategic content placement raises questions about training data integrity. If companies can optimise their content to be disproportionately represented in training data, the resulting models may reflect commercial interests rather than objective reality, potentially compromising the trustworthiness that makes these systems valuable in the first place.

The digital marketing measurement industry will also need to evolve. Current analytics tools are designed around click-through rates, search rankings, and website traffic — metrics that may not capture the value of an AI brand mention that never generates a click but influences a purchase decision made entirely within a chat interface.

Expert Perspective

Search industry veterans see AI SEO as a natural evolution of the optimisation discipline, noting that every new information retrieval system eventually attracts efforts to influence its outputs. The key question, experts suggest, is not whether AI responses can be influenced — they clearly can — but whether the influence techniques will scale, persist, and remain effective as AI systems evolve their defences.

Some analysts argue that AI SEO may actually be more difficult to game sustainably than traditional search. Large language models synthesise information from vast numbers of sources, making it harder for any single optimisation effort to have a decisive impact. Others counter that the opacity of AI systems — the fact that users cannot see why a model chose to mention one brand over another — makes manipulation harder to detect and therefore potentially more persistent.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses navigating the AI search landscape, the practical advice remains grounded in fundamentals: ensure your brand information is accurate, consistent, and widely distributed across authoritative sources. This includes maintaining comprehensive product documentation, earning genuine reviews and mentions, and building the kind of authentic online presence that both traditional search engines and AI systems recognise as trustworthy.

Companies selling software and digital products should pay particular attention to how AI systems describe and recommend products in their category. Ensuring that product pages, knowledge bases, and support documentation clearly communicate value propositions helps AI models accurately represent your offerings. For those selling affordable Microsoft Office licence keys or genuine Windows 11 key products, clear and authoritative product information becomes even more important as AI increasingly mediates purchasing decisions. Businesses should view their presence through trusted enterprise productivity software platforms as a key part of their AI-age visibility strategy.

Key Takeaways

  • The SEO industry is actively developing techniques to influence AI search responses from Google, ChatGPT, and other platforms
  • Some agencies report measurable success in increasing AI brand mentions for clients
  • AI responses carry more implied authority than traditional search results, raising manipulation concerns
  • No current disclosure mechanism exists to indicate commercially influenced AI recommendations
  • Google and AI developers face new challenges in preventing systematic gaming of their systems
  • Businesses should focus on authentic, widely distributed brand information as their best AI optimisation strategy

Looking Ahead

The AI SEO landscape will evolve rapidly over the coming months as more data becomes available about which techniques actually work and which are marketing hype. Expect AI platform providers to develop countermeasures similar to Google's historical algorithm updates — periodic changes designed to neutralise manipulation techniques. Regulatory bodies may also weigh in, potentially requiring AI platforms to disclose when responses have been influenced by commercial optimisation. For now, the wisest approach for businesses is to invest in genuine authority and quality rather than chasing speculative AI manipulation shortcuts.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AI SEO?

AI SEO, or generative engine optimisation, refers to techniques designed to influence how AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Gemini reference and recommend brands in their responses. It represents a shift from optimising for search rankings to optimising for AI mentions.

Can AI search results really be manipulated?

According to industry reports, some agencies claim measurable success in increasing AI brand mentions through strategic content placement and data optimisation. However, the field is young and measurement methodologies remain unproven at scale.

How should businesses prepare for AI search?

Focus on ensuring your brand information is accurate, consistent, and widely distributed across authoritative sources. Maintain comprehensive product documentation, earn genuine reviews, and build an authentic online presence that both search engines and AI systems recognise as trustworthy.
