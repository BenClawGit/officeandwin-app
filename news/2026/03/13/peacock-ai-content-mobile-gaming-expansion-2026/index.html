Peacock Bets Big on AI-Generated Content and Mobile Gaming to Drive Subscriber Growth

NBCUniversal's streaming platform is deploying artificial intelligence, mobile-first gaming, and algorithmic content curation in an ambitious bid to differentiate itself in an increasingly crowded market.

What Happened

Peacock, NBCUniversal's streaming service, announced a sweeping expansion strategy centred on AI-powered video experiences, mobile gaming integration, and new content formats designed for smartphone-first consumption. The announcement signals a fundamental shift in how Peacock plans to compete against dominant rivals like Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime Video.

Among the most eye-catching initiatives is the introduction of an AI-generated avatar of television host Andy Cohen, who will serve as a virtual guide through an algorithmically curated feed of clips from Bravo shows including The Real Housewives and Love Island. The AI Cohen will narrate and contextualise an endless, swipeable stream of short-form content designed to mimic the addictive scroll mechanics popularised by TikTok and Instagram Reels.

The platform is also rolling out native vertical video formatting for live sports, beginning with NBA games this spring. Using computer vision algorithms to track players and dynamically reframe the action, Peacock will allow viewers to watch live basketball in portrait mode without manually rotating their phones. This represents a significant technical investment in real-time video processing at scale.

Background and Context

Peacock has struggled to establish a distinct identity in the streaming wars since its launch in 2020. While NBCUniversal's content library is substantial — spanning NBC broadcast content, Universal films, and premium cable networks — the platform has consistently ranked behind Netflix, Amazon, Disney+, and even Max in subscriber count and cultural mindshare.

The streaming industry as a whole has entered a maturation phase where subscriber growth has slowed and profitability has become the primary metric of success. Netflix achieved consistent profitability through content investment discipline and advertising tiers. Disney+ has pursued aggressive bundling with Hulu and ESPN+. Amazon leverages Prime Video as a loss leader within its broader ecosystem.

Peacock's challenge is that it lacks the scale advantages of these competitors. NBCUniversal parent Comcast has been selective about investment levels, and the platform has needed to find creative — and cost-efficient — ways to grow. AI-generated content and algorithmic curation offer a potentially attractive economics model: producing engaging content at a fraction of the cost of traditional scripted programming.

The vertical sports video feature responds to a genuine shift in media consumption. Mobile viewing now accounts for the majority of streaming minutes for users under 35, and the industry has been slow to adapt its production formats to portrait-mode screens.

Why This Matters

Peacock's AI push represents one of the most aggressive deployments of generative AI in mainstream entertainment to date. While other platforms have used AI for recommendation engines and content metadata tagging, creating an AI avatar of a real television personality to host an infinite content feed crosses into genuinely new territory.

This matters because it tests whether audiences will accept AI-generated hosts as substitutes for authentic human presentation. Andy Cohen's appeal is rooted in his personality, spontaneity, and cultural commentary — qualities that current AI systems can approximate but not genuinely replicate. If audiences embrace AI Cohen, it could accelerate the adoption of synthetic presenters across the media industry, with profound implications for talent, labour relations, and content authenticity.

The vertical sports streaming initiative is arguably more consequential from a technology standpoint. Real-time algorithmic reframing of live video requires sophisticated computer vision running at broadcast speeds with minimal latency. If Peacock executes this successfully at scale during NBA games, it could establish a new standard for mobile sports consumption that competitors will need to match. For businesses and individuals managing their digital workflows through tools like an affordable Microsoft Office licence, the AI-everywhere trend in entertainment mirrors the productivity AI revolution transforming how office work gets done.

Industry Impact

The streaming industry will be watching Peacock's AI experiment closely as a bellwether for the economics of AI-generated content. If an AI host can drive meaningful engagement metrics at a fraction of the cost of traditional production, every streaming platform will face pressure to develop similar capabilities.

For the sports broadcasting industry, vertical video represents a potential inflection point. Traditional sports broadcasts are designed for landscape screens — cameras, graphics packages, and production workflows all assume a 16:9 aspect ratio. Peacock's algorithmic reframing approach, if successful, avoids the need to restructure entire production pipelines and instead solves the problem computationally. This could make vertical sports viewing a standard feature across all major platforms within two to three years.

The gaming integration component adds another dimension to the competitive landscape. As streaming platforms increasingly compete for total screen time rather than just viewing time, bundling games with video content creates stickier subscription relationships. This follows the model that Apple has explored with Apple Arcade and Apple TV+ bundling, and that Amazon pursues through Prime Gaming.

Content creators and talent agencies should note the precedent being set with AI avatars. The licensing terms under which Andy Cohen's likeness was used for AI generation will likely become a reference point for future negotiations between performers and platforms.

Expert Perspective

Peacock's strategy reflects a pragmatic assessment of its competitive position. Rather than attempting to outspend Netflix on original scripted content — a battle it cannot win — Peacock is betting on format innovation and cost-efficient AI content to carve out a differentiated niche.

The risk lies in execution and audience reception. AI-generated content that feels cheap or inauthentic could damage brand perception rather than enhance it. The vertical sports feature, while technically impressive, could also frustrate viewers who prefer the context provided by wide-angle coverage of full-court basketball action.

However, the willingness to experiment aggressively is itself valuable. In a maturing market where most platforms are playing it safe to protect profitability, Peacock's willingness to try genuinely novel formats could yield unexpected hits that reshade competitive dynamics.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses evaluating AI deployment strategies, Peacock's approach offers useful lessons about using AI to create new customer experiences rather than simply automating existing processes. The principle of using AI to offer something genuinely new — rather than a cheaper version of something old — applies across industries from entertainment to enterprise productivity software.

Companies investing in digital transformation should also note the vertical video trend. Marketing teams producing video content should increasingly consider portrait-mode formats for mobile-first audiences. Similarly, businesses running internal communications through platforms like Microsoft Teams should consider how mobile consumption patterns affect content engagement. Looking Ahead

The AI Cohen feature and vertical NBA streams are expected to launch this spring, providing the first real-world test of audience appetite for these formats. Engagement metrics from the initial rollout will likely determine whether Peacock doubles down on AI content creation or pivots to alternative growth strategies. The broader streaming industry will be watching closely — a successful debut could trigger a wave of similar AI initiatives across competing platforms within months.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Peacock's new AI feature?

Peacock is creating an AI-generated avatar of TV host Andy Cohen that will narrate and guide viewers through an algorithmically curated, infinitely scrollable feed of clips from Bravo shows like The Real Housewives and Love Island.

How does Peacock's vertical sports streaming work?

Using computer vision algorithms that track players in real-time, Peacock dynamically reframes live NBA broadcasts into portrait mode so viewers can watch on their phones without rotating the screen.

By bundling gaming with video content, Peacock aims to increase total screen time and create stickier subscription relationships, following a model similar to Apple Arcade and Amazon Prime Gaming.
