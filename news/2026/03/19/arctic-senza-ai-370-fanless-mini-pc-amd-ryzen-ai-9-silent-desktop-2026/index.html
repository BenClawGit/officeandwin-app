Arctic Launches $1,400 Fanless Mini-PC With AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370 for Silent AI Workloads

What Happened

Arctic, the Swiss company best known for its PC cooling solutions, has entered the mini-PC market with the Senza AI 370 — a completely fanless desktop computer powered by AMD's Ryzen AI 9 HX 370 processor. Priced at approximately $1,400, the system ships with 32GB of fast DDR5 RAM, a 1TB NVMe SSD, and a comprehensive port selection, all housed in a passively cooled chassis designed to mount discreetly under a desk.

The Senza AI 370's defining characteristic is its complete absence of moving parts. Arctic has leveraged its extensive thermal engineering expertise to design a case that doubles as a massive heatsink, dissipating the heat generated by the Ryzen AI 9 HX 370's 54-watt thermal design power through convection alone. The result is a computer that produces zero acoustic noise — a significant differentiator for professionals who work in noise-sensitive environments.

The system's AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370 processor is a Strix Point-generation chip featuring Zen 5 CPU cores, RDNA 3.5 integrated graphics, and a dedicated XDNA 2 neural processing unit (NPU) capable of up to 50 TOPS of AI inference performance. This combination makes the Senza AI 370 one of the first fanless systems specifically positioned for local AI workloads.

Background and Context

The mini-PC market has experienced remarkable growth over the past three years, driven by remote work trends, increasing desktop PC capabilities in compact form factors, and growing demand for space-efficient computing solutions. Companies like Intel NUC (before its dissolution), Minisforum, Beelink, and Geekom have expanded the category from niche to mainstream, offering capable desktop replacements at competitive price points.

Arctic's entry is distinctive because of its thermal engineering heritage. The company has been manufacturing CPU coolers, case fans, and thermal compounds since 2001, giving it deep expertise in heat management — the primary engineering challenge of fanless computing. Most fanless PCs on the market use lower-power processors to stay within thermal limits, but Arctic's design accommodates a high-performance mobile processor by using the entire chassis as a cooling surface.

The integration of AMD's XDNA 2 NPU is strategically significant. As AI features become standard across enterprise productivity software — from document summarization in Microsoft Office to image generation in creative tools — the ability to run these workloads locally rather than in the cloud offers benefits in latency, privacy, and cost. The NPU handles AI inference tasks without burdening the CPU or GPU, enabling simultaneous AI processing and traditional computing workloads.

Why This Matters

The Senza AI 370 represents a convergence of several important technology trends: the maturation of passively cooled high-performance computing, the integration of dedicated AI processing hardware into consumer and professional systems, and the miniaturization of desktop computing into form factors that disappear into work environments.

For professionals in noise-sensitive fields — recording studios, medical offices, libraries, courtrooms, and open-plan offices — a completely silent computer removes a constant source of ambient noise that, while individually minor, contributes meaningfully to fatigue and distraction over the course of a workday. The Senza AI 370 is arguably the most powerful fanless system commercially available, offering performance comparable to many traditional desktop systems while producing no sound whatsoever.

The AI angle is equally significant. Microsoft's push for "Copilot+ PCs" — systems that meet minimum AI processing requirements for next-generation Windows features — has made NPU capability a relevant specification for mainstream buyers. The Ryzen AI 9 HX 370's 50 TOPS NPU performance exceeds Microsoft's Copilot+ PC threshold, meaning the Senza AI 370 qualifies for features like Live Captions, Windows Studio Effects, and advanced Recall capabilities when paired with a genuine Windows 11 key.

Industry Impact

Arctic's entry into the mini-PC market signals that established component manufacturers see viable business opportunities in complete system integration. The company's cooling expertise gives it a genuine technical moat in the fanless segment — designing effective passive cooling for 54-watt processors requires thermal engineering capabilities that generic mini-PC assemblers typically lack.

The $1,400 price point positions the Senza AI 370 as a premium product in the mini-PC category, where capable systems from competitors start at $300-$500. Arctic is betting that the combination of zero noise, AI capability, and build quality justifies the premium — a bet that targets professionals and enthusiasts rather than budget-conscious consumers.

For AMD, the Senza AI 370 represents a validation of the Strix Point platform's versatility. AMD's strategy of integrating CPU, GPU, and NPU capabilities into a single chip enables system designs that would be impossible with discrete components, and fanless systems represent one of the most compelling demonstrations of this integration advantage.

The broader trend toward AI-capable PCs will accelerate as more software leverages NPU hardware. Microsoft, Adobe, and other major software vendors are actively developing features that utilize local AI processing, and the availability of capable hardware like the Senza AI 370 — paired with properly licensed software like an affordable Microsoft Office licence — creates a virtuous cycle of adoption.

Expert Perspective

Hardware reviewers and thermal engineers have expressed admiration for Arctic's design ambitions while noting the inherent tradeoffs of fanless cooling. Passive cooling works well in controlled environments with reasonable ambient temperatures, but performance may throttle in hot climates or enclosed spaces with poor ventilation. The Ryzen AI 9 HX 370's boost clocks may not sustain under prolonged heavy loads, meaning the Senza AI 370 may perform differently in benchmarks versus sustained workloads.

The under-desk mounting design is clever because it positions the chassis in a typically cooler location while keeping it out of sight. However, it also places the system in a location where airflow may be restricted by walls, cable management, and other under-desk equipment. Arctic's real-world thermal performance will be a key focus of upcoming hardware reviews.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses equipping knowledge workers, the Senza AI 370 offers a compelling proposition for specific use cases. Medical offices, legal firms, creative studios, and any environment where noise is undesirable can benefit from completely silent computing without sacrificing modern performance. The AI processing capability future-proofs the investment as AI-powered productivity features become standard across enterprise software.

The total cost of ownership calculation should include the system's longevity advantage: without fans, the primary wear component in most computers is eliminated, potentially extending the useful life of the system beyond typical desktop refresh cycles.

Looking Ahead

Arctic's entry into complete systems could signal a broader trend of component manufacturers moving up the value chain into integrated products. If the Senza AI 370 proves commercially successful, expect Arctic to expand the line with different processor options and form factors. The fanless AI-capable PC segment is likely to grow as NPU-optimized software becomes more prevalent and professionals increasingly demand silent computing environments that don't compromise on capability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Arctic Senza AI 370?

The Arctic Senza AI 370 is a $1,400 completely fanless mini-PC powered by AMD's Ryzen AI 9 HX 370 processor with 32GB DDR5 RAM, 1TB SSD, and zero acoustic noise through passive cooling design.

Can a fanless PC handle AI workloads?

Yes, the Senza AI 370 includes AMD's XDNA 2 neural processing unit with 50 TOPS of AI inference performance, capable of running local AI workloads including Microsoft Copilot+ PC features without requiring cloud processing.

Is a fanless PC reliable?
