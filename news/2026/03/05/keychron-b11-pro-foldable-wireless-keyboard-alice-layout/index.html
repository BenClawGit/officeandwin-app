Keychron Unveils B11 Pro Foldable Wireless Keyboard With Ergonomic Alice Layout for $65

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-05

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Keychron launches B11 Pro foldable wireless keyboard with ergonomic Alice layout at $64.99
  • Features Bluetooth, USB-C, and 2.4GHz wireless with full key remapping via web app
  • Targets mobile professionals needing ergonomic typing across multiple work locations
  • Priced for both individual purchase and enterprise fleet deployment

What Happened

Keychron, the keyboard manufacturer that has built a devoted following among mechanical keyboard enthusiasts and remote workers, has announced the B11 Pro — a wireless keyboard that folds in half for portability while maintaining the ergonomic split Alice layout favored by users who prioritize typing comfort. Priced at $64.99, the B11 Pro launched on March 4, 2026, and immediately drew attention for its unusual combination of portability, ergonomics, and customization at an accessible price point.

The B11 Pro features concave ABS keycaps with a low-profile scissor mechanism — a departure from the mechanical switches that Keychron is best known for, traded here for the thinness required to make the folding form factor practical. Connectivity options include Bluetooth, USB-C wired, and 2.4GHz wireless via a dongle, covering virtually every connection scenario. A 250mAh battery provides wireless operation, and the keyboard can be fully remapped and customized through Keychron's Launcher web application.

The announcement follows Keychron's CES 2026 reveal of the Nape Pro, a compact trackball that signaled the company's expansion beyond traditional keyboards into broader input device categories. The B11 Pro reinforces this trajectory, targeting mobile professionals who need a full typing experience that can fit into a bag alongside their laptop, tablet, or smartphone.

Background and Context

The market for portable keyboards has grown substantially alongside the rise of remote and hybrid work arrangements. Professionals who split their time between home offices, coworking spaces, coffee shops, and traditional offices need input devices that can travel with them without adding excessive bulk or weight. Traditional full-size keyboards are impractical for mobile use, while most portable keyboards sacrifice ergonomics for compactness.

The Alice layout — a split, angled keyboard design that reduces wrist strain by aligning key columns more naturally with the angles of human forearms — has gained significant popularity among ergonomics-conscious typists. Named after a community-designed keyboard project, the Alice layout typically requires a larger form factor due to the split design, making Keychron's achievement of fitting it into a foldable package particularly noteworthy.

Keychron has built its reputation by democratizing the custom keyboard market, offering well-built mechanical keyboards at price points far below the boutique custom keyboard scene while maintaining the quality and customization options that enthusiasts demand. The B11 Pro extends this philosophy to the portable segment, offering a product that would typically command a premium price at a mass-market $64.99 price point.

The broader accessory market is experiencing a renaissance driven by the diversification of computing platforms. Users now regularly switch between laptops, tablets, smartphones, and desktop setups — often running different operating systems — and need peripherals that work seamlessly across all of them. The B11 Pro's multi-device connectivity addresses this reality directly.

Why This Matters

The B11 Pro represents a convergence of trends that are reshaping how people interact with technology in professional settings. As remote work becomes permanent for millions of workers who use tools like enterprise productivity software across multiple devices and locations, the demand for portable accessories that don't compromise on ergonomics or functionality is growing rapidly.

Ergonomic considerations are increasingly front-of-mind for both individual workers and organizations. Repetitive strain injuries from keyboard use cost employers billions annually in lost productivity and workers' compensation claims, and the ergonomic benefits of split keyboard layouts are well-documented in occupational health literature. A foldable split keyboard that's practical for daily carry could help workers maintain healthy typing posture regardless of where they're working.

Industry Impact

The keyboard accessory market is being reshaped by products like the B11 Pro that refuse to accept the traditional tradeoffs between portability, ergonomics, and price. Competitors including Logitech, Microsoft, and Apple will need to evaluate their own portable keyboard offerings against Keychron's combination of features, noting that the B11 Pro offers a split ergonomic layout, multi-device connectivity, and full remappability at a price point that undercuts many basic portable keyboards from major brands.

For professionals who pair portable keyboards with productivity software like affordable Microsoft Office licences, the B11 Pro's remappability through Keychron's Launcher web app is particularly valuable. Users can create custom key mappings optimized for specific applications — Excel shortcuts, Word formatting commands, Teams navigation — that travel with them across devices.

The USB-C, Bluetooth, and 2.4GHz wireless connectivity trifecta also sets a new baseline expectation for peripheral connectivity. As workplaces increasingly standardize on USB-C and wireless protocols, accessories that support all major connection methods offer the flexibility that modern multi-device workflows demand.

Enterprise procurement teams evaluating bulk keyboard purchases for hybrid workforces should take note. At $64.99, the B11 Pro is priced within the typical per-employee accessory budget for most organizations, making ergonomic split keyboards accessible for fleet deployment rather than limiting them to special accommodation requests.

Expert Perspective

The engineering challenge of creating a foldable keyboard that maintains good typing feel and ergonomic benefits is non-trivial. The hinge mechanism must be robust enough for daily folding and unfolding, the key alignment must remain precise when the keyboard is unfolded, and the split angle must be consistent to provide reliable ergonomic benefits. Keychron's decision to use low-profile scissor switches rather than its signature mechanical switches reflects a pragmatic engineering choice — mechanical switches are simply too thick for a form factor that needs to fold flat.

The $64.99 price point is also strategically important. It positions the B11 Pro as an impulse-friendly purchase for professionals who might not otherwise invest in a separate portable keyboard, potentially expanding the total addressable market for the category rather than just capturing share from existing competitors.

What This Means for Businesses

Organizations with hybrid or remote workforces should evaluate the B11 Pro as part of their employee equipment standards. Running genuine Windows 11 keys on company machines paired with ergonomic accessories can reduce workplace injury claims while improving employee productivity and satisfaction. The combination of portability, ergonomics, and affordability makes the B11 Pro a strong candidate for inclusion in remote work equipment packages.

IT departments will appreciate the web-based remapping capability, which allows standardized key configurations to be deployed without installing software on managed devices. This reduces compatibility concerns and simplifies deployment across mixed-OS environments.

As remote work patterns continue to evolve, the market for high-quality portable accessories that prioritize both ergonomics and functionality is poised for sustained growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of switches does the Keychron B11 Pro use?

The B11 Pro uses low-profile scissor switches rather than mechanical switches. This design choice enables the thin foldable form factor while still providing a comfortable typing experience.

Can I customize the key layout?

Yes, the B11 Pro supports full remapping through Keychron's Launcher web application. You can create custom key mappings for different applications and workflows.

How does the B11 Pro connect to devices?

The keyboard offers three connectivity options: Bluetooth for wireless pairing with multiple devices, USB-C for wired connection, and 2.4GHz wireless via an included dongle for the lowest latency wireless experience.
