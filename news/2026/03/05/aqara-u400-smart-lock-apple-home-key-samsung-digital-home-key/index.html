Aqara U400 Becomes First Smart Lock to Support Both Apple Home Key and Samsung Digital Home Key

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-05

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Aqara U400 becomes first smart lock to support both Apple Home Key and Samsung Digital Home Key
  • Dual-platform support delivered via firmware update — no hardware changes needed
  • Eliminates ecosystem lock-in for mixed iPhone/Samsung households and businesses
  • Sets new compatibility benchmark for the entire smart lock industry

What Happened

The Aqara U400 smart lock has achieved a notable first in the smart home industry: it is now the first smart lock to support both Apple's Home Key and Samsung's recently launched Digital Home Key standard. The update, confirmed on March 4, 2026, means that the U400 can be unlocked hands-free using either an iPhone with Apple Wallet or a Samsung Galaxy device with Samsung Wallet, making it the most universally compatible premium smart lock on the market.

The U400, which originally launched earlier in 2026 with Apple Home Key and UWB (Ultra-Wideband) hands-free unlocking support, has received a firmware update that adds Samsung's Digital Home Key compatibility. This dual-platform support eliminates one of the most significant friction points in smart lock adoption: the need to choose a lock that works with your specific smartphone ecosystem. Mixed-platform households — where some members use iPhones and others use Samsung Galaxy devices — can now standardize on a single lock.

Samsung's Digital Home Key is a relatively new standard that brings tap-to-unlock and proximity-based hands-free unlocking to Samsung Wallet, mirroring the functionality that Apple users have enjoyed through Home Key since its introduction. The Aqara U400's rapid adoption of the Samsung standard demonstrates the manufacturer's commitment to cross-platform compatibility and positions the company as a leader in the increasingly fragmented smart home landscape.

Background and Context

The smart lock market has been growing steadily, driven by consumer demand for keyless entry, remote access management, and integration with broader smart home ecosystems. However, adoption has been hampered by ecosystem fragmentation — locks that work seamlessly with Apple's HomeKit may have limited functionality with Google Home or Samsung SmartThings, forcing consumers to make purchasing decisions based on their smartphone platform rather than the lock's features and quality.

Apple introduced Home Key in iOS 15, allowing compatible smart locks to be unlocked by holding an iPhone or Apple Watch near the lock. The technology uses NFC for tap-to-unlock and UWB for hands-free proximity unlocking, providing a seamless experience that eliminates the need to pull out a phone, open an app, or even wake the device's screen. Samsung's Digital Home Key, announced in early 2026, brings equivalent functionality to the Galaxy ecosystem.

Aqara has positioned itself as one of the more platform-agnostic smart home manufacturers, supporting Apple HomeKit, Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Samsung SmartThings across its product line. The company's Zigbee-based ecosystem has allowed it to maintain broad compatibility while many competitors have tied their products more closely to specific platforms. The U400's dual Home Key support extends this philosophy to the most premium tier of smart lock interaction.

The broader smart home industry is in the early stages of a consolidation around the Matter standard, which promises unified interoperability across platforms. However, premium features like Home Key and Digital Home Key operate at a layer above Matter's base functionality, meaning that platform-specific compatibility remains an important differentiator for high-end devices.

Why This Matters

Universal compatibility in smart home devices addresses one of the biggest barriers to adoption: the fear of platform lock-in. When consumers invest in smart home infrastructure — which often requires professional installation and represents a significant financial commitment — the concern that a future change in smartphone platform will render their devices less functional is a legitimate deterrent. The Aqara U400's support for both major digital key platforms eliminates this concern for the most visible and frequently used smart home device: the front door lock.

For households and businesses that use a mix of technology platforms alongside enterprise productivity software from different vendors, the U400 represents the kind of cross-platform thinking that consumers increasingly expect from their technology purchases. The era of choosing hardware based solely on which smartphone you happen to carry is giving way to a demand for devices that work universally well across platforms.

The security implications are also noteworthy. Digital keys stored in secure hardware enclaves (Apple's Secure Element, Samsung's Knox) offer stronger authentication than traditional PIN codes, Bluetooth-based app unlocking, or physical keys that can be copied. By supporting both platforms' most secure key mechanisms, the U400 provides enterprise-grade security that adapts to whichever device the user carries.

Industry Impact

The U400's dual Home Key support sets a new benchmark that competing smart lock manufacturers will be pressured to match. Yale, Schlage, August, and other major lock brands will need to accelerate their own support for Samsung's Digital Home Key — and maintain robust Apple Home Key integration — or risk being perceived as less capable alternatives.

For property management companies and businesses that manage access across multiple locations, the U400's universal compatibility is particularly compelling. Properties with diverse tenant bases — where residents use a mix of iPhone and Samsung devices — can deploy a single lock model without worrying about platform-specific limitations. This simplifies procurement, reduces support overhead, and provides a consistent experience for all users.

Companies offering digital products like affordable Microsoft Office licences understand the value of cross-platform compatibility, and the smart home market is increasingly adopting the same philosophy. The trend toward universal device compatibility — exemplified by the Matter standard and now Aqara's dual Home Key support — will accelerate as consumers reject products that limit their ecosystem flexibility.

The real estate sector should also take note. Smart locks with universal digital key support are becoming a selling point for tech-forward properties, particularly in the luxury rental and serviced apartment markets where tenant convenience and security are premium differentiators.

Expert Perspective

Aqara's ability to ship dual Home Key support through a firmware update — rather than requiring new hardware — demonstrates the advantage of designing smart home devices with future compatibility in mind. The U400's hardware was apparently designed with sufficient NFC and processing capabilities to accommodate additional digital key standards as they emerged, a forward-thinking approach that many competitors' current-generation products may not have anticipated.

This also highlights the importance of choosing smart home devices from manufacturers with demonstrated commitment to ongoing software support. The smart home market is littered with products that worked well at launch but became obsolete when new standards emerged and manufacturers failed to provide updates. Aqara's rapid adoption of Samsung's Digital Home Key suggests a software support philosophy that prioritizes longevity.

What This Means for Businesses

Commercial property managers and businesses evaluating access control solutions should strongly consider the Aqara U400 for any location where occupants use a mix of Apple and Samsung devices. The combination of dual Home Key support, UWB hands-free unlocking, and compatibility with major smart home platforms makes it one of the most versatile options available. Organizations standardizing on systems with genuine Windows 11 keys for their computing infrastructure can pair the U400 with broader smart building systems for comprehensive facility management.

Small business owners who want to eliminate the hassle of physical key management — cutting keys for employees, changing locks when staff turns over, dealing with lockouts — can implement the U400 as a digital-first access solution that works regardless of which smartphone platform their employees use.

Looking Ahead

Expect other smart lock manufacturers to announce Samsung Digital Home Key support in the coming months as the standard gains traction. The Matter standard's continued evolution may eventually subsume platform-specific digital key implementations into a unified standard, but until that happens, multi-platform support like the U400 offers remains the gold standard for consumer convenience. Aqara is likely to extend dual Home Key support to additional products in its lineup, potentially including smart deadbolts and commercial-grade access control systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes the Aqara U400 special?

The U400 is the first smart lock that supports both Apple's Home Key and Samsung's Digital Home Key, allowing hands-free or tap-to-unlock from either an iPhone or Samsung Galaxy device.

Do I need to buy new hardware for Samsung Digital Home Key?

No. Existing Aqara U400 owners receive Samsung Digital Home Key support through a firmware update, requiring no hardware replacement.

Does the U400 work with Google phones?

The U400 supports Google Home and standard Bluetooth unlocking for non-Apple/Samsung devices. However, the premium digital key tap-to-unlock and hands-free features are currently limited to Apple Home Key and Samsung Digital Home Key.
