Anker Soundcore P31i Earbuds Deliver Premium Features at $40 — A Serious AirPods Alternative

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-09

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Anker Soundcore P31i earbuds deliver ANC, transparency mode, and long battery life at just $40
  • ZDNet reviewer replaced AirPods with the budget earbuds without regretting the switch
  • Chipset commoditisation has nearly eliminated the feature gap between budget and premium earbuds
  • Premium brands face growing pressure as budget alternatives gain mainstream credibility

What Happened

Anker's audio brand Soundcore has released the P31i true wireless earbuds at a remarkably aggressive $40 price point, and early reviews suggest they represent a genuine threat to earbuds costing three to five times as much. ZDNet's reviewer went so far as to replace their Apple AirPods with the P31i for daily use — and reported no regrets. The budget earbuds deliver active noise cancellation, transparency mode, long battery life, and surprisingly capable sound quality in a compact form factor.

The P31i arrives at a time when the true wireless earbuds market has matured significantly. Features that were once exclusive to premium models from Apple, Sony, and Bose have cascaded down to budget products, driven by commoditisation of Bluetooth audio chipsets and miniaturised ANC hardware. Anker has been at the forefront of this democratisation, consistently delivering products that punch well above their price point.

What makes the P31i particularly noteworthy is the completeness of its feature set. At $40, buyers get active noise cancellation with multiple modes, ambient sound passthrough, customisable EQ through the Soundcore app, IPX5 water resistance, and battery life that exceeds many premium competitors. The earbuds also include AI-enhanced call quality, using machine learning to isolate the wearer's voice from background noise during phone calls.

Background and Context

The true wireless earbuds market has undergone a dramatic transformation since Apple popularised the category with the original AirPods in 2016. What began as a premium luxury has become a commodity product category, with hundreds of options available at every price point. The global market is projected to exceed $100 billion by 2028, driven by the dual trends of smartphone manufacturers removing headphone jacks and the growing popularity of audio content including podcasts, audiobooks, and music streaming.

Anker's Soundcore brand has built a reputation as the leading value-oriented audio brand, consistently winning 'best budget' recommendations from major technology publications. The company's strategy of delivering 80-90% of premium features at 20-30% of premium prices has proven highly effective, particularly among cost-conscious consumers who refuse to pay Apple or Sony premiums for daily-use accessories.

The ANC technology that drives much of the P31i's appeal has followed a Moore's Law-like trajectory of cost reduction. Active noise cancellation requires dedicated processing hardware, multiple microphones, and sophisticated algorithms — all of which have become dramatically cheaper as the technology matures. Qualcomm and MediaTek's latest Bluetooth audio chipsets integrate ANC capability directly into the SoC, eliminating the need for separate ANC processors and reducing manufacturing costs.

Why This Matters

The Soundcore P31i represents a broader trend that has significant implications for the technology accessories market: the effective elimination of the feature gap between budget and premium products. When a $40 product delivers ANC, transparency mode, long battery life, and good sound quality, the justification for spending $179-$299 on premium alternatives narrows to brand preference, ecosystem integration, and marginal quality improvements.

This price compression is particularly relevant for businesses and professionals who need reliable audio equipment for remote work, conference calls, and focused productivity. The cost of equipping an entire team with capable earbuds has dropped dramatically — the same budget that would buy AirPods Pro for three employees can now provide ANC earbuds to an entire department. Combined with enterprise productivity software like Microsoft Teams and Zoom, affordable audio equipment removes one of the remaining barriers to effective remote collaboration.

For consumers, the P31i and products like it represent genuine progress in making technology more accessible. Not everyone can afford $250 earbuds, but nearly everyone can find $40. Democratising features like noise cancellation — which has real benefits for focus, productivity, and hearing protection — is a meaningful improvement in technology equity.

Industry Impact

Apple, Sony, and Bose face increasing pressure to justify premium pricing as budget alternatives close the quality gap. The strategy of relying on brand cachet and marginal quality advantages becomes less viable as reviewers from mainstream publications openly recommend budget alternatives over premium incumbents. The risk for premium brands is not that they lose all customers to budget alternatives, but that the consideration set expands to include budget options that weren't previously competitive.

The earbuds market is likely to bifurcate more sharply: a premium tier that competes on ecosystem integration (Apple's seamless iPhone pairing, for example), best-in-class ANC performance, and materials quality, versus a value tier that delivers 90% of the experience at a fraction of the cost. The middle ground — $100-150 earbuds that aren't clearly premium or budget — is being squeezed out.

For accessory manufacturers, the race to the bottom in pricing is challenging for margins but positive for unit volume. Companies like Anker that have optimised their supply chains and manufacturing processes for high-volume, low-margin products are well-positioned to benefit. The earbuds market mirrors the broader consumer electronics trend where consumers increasingly pair budget accessories with premium devices — using $40 earbuds with a $1,000 smartphone, or affordable peripherals with machines running a genuine Windows 11 key.

Expert Perspective

Audio engineers note that the perceived quality gap between budget and premium earbuds has narrowed more in the past two years than in the previous five, driven primarily by improvements in DSP (digital signal processing) algorithms. Modern audio chipsets can compensate for cheaper drivers through software processing, delivering frequency response and soundstage characteristics that approach premium hardware. The remaining advantages of expensive earbuds are primarily in build quality, durability, and the very best ANC implementations.

Consumer electronics analysts highlight that Anker's vertical integration — from component sourcing through manufacturing to direct-to-consumer sales — gives it structural cost advantages that most competitors cannot match. The company's ability to sell capable ANC earbuds profitably at $40 reflects manufacturing efficiency rather than margin sacrifice.

What This Means for Businesses

IT procurement teams should reconsider their audio equipment budgets. Products like the Soundcore P31i demonstrate that effective ANC earbuds for meetings, calls, and focused work no longer require premium budgets. Standardising on affordable audio equipment and redirecting savings toward software — such as an affordable Microsoft Office licence — may deliver better overall productivity returns than investing in premium headphones.

For professionals and freelancers, the P31i is worth considering as a backup or travel pair even if you own premium earbuds. The low price point makes it practical to keep a pair in your bag, car, or desk drawer for situations where you don't want to risk your more expensive daily drivers.

  • The budget-premium feature gap in earbuds has nearly closed thanks to chipset commoditisation
  • Premium brands face growing pressure to justify pricing as budget alternatives gain credibility
  • Businesses can equip entire teams with capable ANC earbuds at the cost of a few premium pairs
  • AI-enhanced call quality uses machine learning to improve voice clarity during calls

Looking Ahead

The Soundcore P31i is not an outlier — it is the vanguard of a new normal in consumer audio. Expect budget earbuds to continue closing the remaining quality gaps with premium products, particularly in ANC performance and build quality. Within two to three years, the functional difference between a $40 and $250 pair of earbuds may be imperceptible to all but the most discerning audiophiles. Premium brands will need to innovate in new directions — health monitoring, spatial audio, and deeper ecosystem integration — to maintain their pricing power.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the Soundcore P31i earbuds as good as AirPods?

While AirPods offer superior Apple ecosystem integration and marginally better build quality, the P31i delivers comparable active noise cancellation, transparency mode, and sound quality at roughly one-fifth the price. For most users, the differences are difficult to perceive in daily use.

Do the Soundcore P31i have noise cancellation?

Yes, the P31i includes active noise cancellation with multiple modes, plus transparency mode for ambient sound passthrough. The ANC is powered by modern Bluetooth audio chipsets that integrate noise cancellation processing directly into the chip.

Why are budget earbuds getting so much better?

The commoditisation of Bluetooth audio chipsets from Qualcomm and MediaTek, which now integrate ANC capability directly into the SoC, has dramatically reduced the cost of implementing premium features. Improved DSP algorithms also compensate for cheaper drivers through software processing.
