Nscale Raises $2 Billion as Former Meta Executives Nick Clegg and Sheryl Sandberg Join Board

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-09

⚡ Quick Summary

  • UK neocloud Nscale raises $2 billion in Series C funding for GPU-as-a-service infrastructure
  • Former Meta executives Nick Clegg and Sheryl Sandberg join the board alongside ex-Yahoo president Susan Decker
  • Company operates data centres across Europe and US, targeting EU data sovereignty opportunities
  • Raise intensifies competition in GPU cloud market against CoreWeave and hyperscale providers

What Happened

UK-based neocloud company Nscale has completed a massive $2 billion Series C funding round while simultaneously announcing three heavyweight board appointments: former British Deputy Prime Minister Sir Nick Clegg, former Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg, and former Yahoo President Susan Decker. The trio brings decades of combined experience in technology leadership, global policy, and corporate governance to a company that has positioned itself as a European alternative to American hyperscale cloud providers.

Nscale operates GPU-as-a-service data centres across several European countries and the United States, providing the computational infrastructure that AI companies need to train and run their models. The $2 billion raise signals continued investor confidence in the AI infrastructure layer, even as questions mount about whether the current pace of AI investment is sustainable.

Clegg, who stepped down from his role as Meta's president of global affairs just over a year ago, brings what Nscale described as 'deep expertise at the intersection of technology, policy, and global affairs.' His departure from Meta was widely seen as necessary to make way for leadership more acceptable to the incoming Trump administration. He is also a General Partner at Hiro Capital, a private equity firm focused on emerging technology including AI, robotics, and spatial computing.

Background and Context

The neocloud sector has emerged as one of the hottest investment categories in technology, driven by insatiable demand for GPU computing power from AI companies. Traditional cloud providers like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud dominate the market, but specialised GPU cloud companies like Nscale, CoreWeave, and Lambda Labs have carved out significant niches by offering purpose-built infrastructure optimised for AI workloads.

Nscale's European positioning is particularly strategic. As data sovereignty regulations tighten across the EU, having data centres on European soil becomes a competitive advantage. Companies subject to GDPR and other privacy regulations increasingly prefer cloud providers that can guarantee data residency within European borders, rather than relying on American providers whose data could theoretically be accessed under US surveillance laws.

The appointment of former Meta executives to Nscale's board reflects a broader trend of senior technology leaders migrating from established platforms to AI infrastructure companies. Sandberg's tenure at Meta from 2008 to 2022 saw her oversee the company's transformation into a global advertising and social media giant. Decker's Yahoo presidency provided experience scaling internet infrastructure during the early days of cloud computing.

Why This Matters

The $2 billion raise underscores a fundamental reality of the current AI boom: the companies building AI models need enormous amounts of computing infrastructure, and providing that infrastructure has become a multi-billion-dollar opportunity. Nscale's ability to attract this level of investment — along with board members of this calibre — suggests that investors see the AI infrastructure build-out as a long-term structural trend rather than a speculative bubble.

For European technology sovereignty, Nscale represents a significant development. The continent has long struggled to produce globally competitive cloud computing companies, with American and Chinese firms dominating the market. A well-funded European GPU cloud provider could help address concerns about dependence on foreign technology infrastructure, particularly for sensitive AI workloads in defence, healthcare, and financial services. Businesses across Europe that rely on enterprise productivity software stand to benefit from having locally hosted, high-performance computing options.

The board appointments also signal how former Big Tech executives view the future of the industry. Rather than joining established competitors or retiring from technology entirely, leaders like Clegg, Sandberg, and Decker are betting on the AI infrastructure layer — suggesting they believe the next wave of technology value creation will occur in the companies that provide the computational foundation for AI rather than in the AI application companies themselves.

Industry Impact

Nscale's raise intensifies competition in an already crowded GPU cloud market. CoreWeave, which has raised billions and is reportedly preparing for an IPO, now faces a strengthened European competitor with deep political connections and institutional credibility. The market is bifurcating between American-focused providers and those targeting the European data sovereignty opportunity.

The appointment of Clegg, a former British Deputy Prime Minister with extensive EU policy experience, gives Nscale a significant advantage in navigating European regulatory environments. As the EU AI Act creates new compliance requirements for AI infrastructure providers, having board-level expertise in technology regulation could prove decisive in winning enterprise customers who need assurance of regulatory compliance.

For businesses evaluating their cloud strategy — whether running workloads on platforms powered by a genuine Windows 11 key or deploying AI models at scale — the expansion of GPU cloud options means more competitive pricing and better geographic coverage. The growing number of well-funded providers creates downward pressure on GPU rental costs, which could accelerate AI adoption across industries.

Expert Perspective

Cloud computing analysts note that the neocloud sector is at an inflection point. The companies that survive and thrive will be those that can offer not just raw computing power, but integrated solutions including networking, storage, and software tooling optimised for AI workloads. Nscale's $2 billion war chest provides resources to invest in this full-stack approach.

Political risk experts highlight the significance of the board composition. Clegg's policy expertise, Sandberg's operational experience, and Decker's financial acumen create a board that can navigate the complex intersection of technology, regulation, and geopolitics that defines the modern cloud computing landscape. This is particularly relevant as governments increasingly view AI infrastructure as a national security asset.

What This Means for Businesses

Organisations evaluating cloud infrastructure for AI workloads now have another well-funded option with strong European credentials. Companies subject to EU data residency requirements should evaluate Nscale's offerings alongside established providers. The increasing competition in the GPU cloud market is likely to drive down costs and improve service levels across the industry.

For businesses managing their productivity stack with tools like an affordable Microsoft Office licence, the growth of AI infrastructure providers signals that AI-powered productivity tools will become more accessible and affordable as the underlying computing costs decrease. The infrastructure build-out happening now will power the next generation of business applications.

Key Takeaways

  • Nscale has raised $2 billion in Series C funding for its GPU cloud infrastructure business
  • Former Meta executives Nick Clegg and Sheryl Sandberg have joined the board alongside ex-Yahoo president Susan Decker
  • The company operates data centres across Europe and the US, positioning it for EU data sovereignty requirements
  • The raise intensifies competition in the neocloud sector against players like CoreWeave
  • Growing GPU cloud competition should drive down AI computing costs for businesses
  • European AI infrastructure investment supports the continent's technology sovereignty ambitions

Looking Ahead

Nscale's combination of substantial funding and heavyweight board talent positions it as a serious contender in the global GPU cloud market. Watch for the company to announce major European data centre expansions and enterprise customer wins in the coming months. The broader neocloud sector consolidation will likely accelerate, with well-funded survivors acquiring smaller players and the market eventually settling into a pattern similar to the traditional cloud computing oligopoly — but with stronger European representation this time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Nscale?

Nscale is a UK-based neocloud company that provides GPU-as-a-service computing infrastructure across European and US data centres, primarily serving AI companies that need large-scale computing power for training and running models.

Why did Nick Clegg join Nscale?

After leaving Meta's presidency of global affairs in early 2025, Clegg brings expertise in technology policy and global affairs to Nscale's board, which is particularly valuable as the company navigates European AI regulations and data sovereignty requirements.

How does Nscale compete with major cloud providers?

Nscale differentiates through purpose-built GPU infrastructure optimised for AI workloads, European data centre locations for data sovereignty compliance, and competitive pricing compared to hyperscale providers like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
