Apple MacBook Air M5 Review Consensus: Small Upgrades Add Up to the Best Ultraportable Laptop Ever Made

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-09

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Apple MacBook Air M5 receives universal acclaim as the best ultraportable laptop ever made
  • M5 chip delivers faster performance with improved CPU, GPU, and Neural Engine for AI tasks
  • Faster storage and 15+ hour battery life enhance everyday productivity
  • Iterative refinement strategy proves more effective than dramatic annual redesigns

What Happened

The Apple MacBook Air 13-inch with the new M5 chip has arrived, and early reviews are unanimous: it is the best ultraportable laptop ever made. The latest iteration brings a faster processor and improved storage performance while maintaining the design philosophy that has made the MacBook Air the world's best-selling laptop. Reviewers who have spent days using the machine as their primary work device report that the incremental improvements, while individually modest, compound into a meaningfully better experience.

The M5 chip delivers measurable performance gains over its M4 predecessor, with reviewers noting faster application launches, smoother multitasking, and improved performance in creative workflows. The storage upgrade to faster NAND flash memory means that everyday operations like opening large files and booting the system feel noticeably snappier. Apple has also maintained the exceptional battery life that has become a MacBook Air hallmark, with real-world usage consistently exceeding 15 hours.

Perhaps most notably, Apple has resisted the temptation to change what works. The industrial design, keyboard, trackpad, and display remain largely unchanged from the M4 model — a deliberate choice that reflects Apple's confidence that these elements have already reached a level of refinement that doesn't require annual redesigns.

Background and Context

The MacBook Air has undergone a remarkable transformation over the past five years. Once positioned as Apple's entry-level laptop with significant performance compromises, the transition to Apple Silicon chips starting with the M1 in 2020 turned the Air into a machine that rivals or exceeds many professional-grade laptops from competitors. The fanless design, all-day battery life, and increasingly powerful processors have made it the default recommendation for the vast majority of laptop buyers.

Apple's M-series chip development cadence has settled into an annual rhythm, with each generation delivering 15-20% performance improvements across CPU and GPU benchmarks. The M5 continues this trajectory, with the Neural Engine seeing particularly significant upgrades to support Apple's expanding AI and machine learning features. These local AI processing capabilities are increasingly important as Apple Intelligence features become more deeply integrated into macOS.

The competitive landscape has intensified, with Qualcomm's Snapdragon X Elite and Intel's latest mobile processors powering increasingly capable Windows ultraportables. However, Apple's vertical integration — designing both the chip and the software — continues to deliver optimisation advantages that competitors struggle to match, particularly in power efficiency and sustained performance.

Why This Matters

The MacBook Air M5 represents a maturation of the laptop market that has significant implications for how people work. When a sub-$1,300 laptop can handle everything from document editing and video conferencing to light video editing and software development, the traditional segmentation between consumer and professional laptops begins to collapse. This is particularly relevant for small businesses and freelancers who need capable machines without enterprise-level budgets.

The improved Neural Engine in the M5 chip signals Apple's commitment to on-device AI processing, a strategy that prioritises user privacy by keeping data local rather than sending it to cloud servers. As AI-powered features become standard in productivity applications — including tools like affordable Microsoft Office licence suites with Copilot integration — having sufficient local processing power to run these features smoothly becomes a critical consideration for laptop buyers.

For the broader technology industry, the MacBook Air's continued dominance demonstrates that iterative improvement of a well-designed product can be more valuable than revolutionary changes. The approach challenges the tech industry's addiction to dramatic annual redesigns and suggests that consumers value reliability, performance, and battery life over aesthetic novelty.

Industry Impact

Windows laptop manufacturers face an increasingly difficult competitive challenge. The MacBook Air M5 sets a benchmark that few Windows ultraportables can match across the full spectrum of performance, battery life, build quality, and software optimisation. Qualcomm's Snapdragon X Elite has narrowed the gap in some benchmarks, but the Windows ARM ecosystem still lacks the software compatibility maturity of Apple's platform.

The laptop market's bifurcation is accelerating: at the high end, Apple's MacBook Air captures the premium ultraportable segment with little serious competition. At the budget end, Chromebooks and affordable Windows laptops serve price-sensitive buyers. The middle ground — $800-1,200 Windows ultraportables — is being squeezed from both sides. Manufacturers in this segment need to offer compelling differentiation, whether through unique form factors, superior displays, or aggressive pricing.

Enterprise IT departments purchasing decisions are also being influenced. As more organisations adopt BYOD policies or provide employees with laptop allowances, the MacBook Air's combination of capabilities and ecosystem integration makes it an increasingly common choice. Organisations running enterprise productivity software on macOS benefit from the platform's stability and security features.

Expert Perspective

Technology analysts highlight that the MacBook Air M5 exemplifies a broader trend in consumer electronics: diminishing returns on annual upgrades. Each generation brings meaningful but modest improvements, making the upgrade decision more about the age of your current device than the excitement of the new one. Users on M3 or older chips will notice substantial improvements; those on M4 may find less reason to upgrade.

Silicon design experts note that Apple's ability to deliver consistent year-over-year improvements in its custom chips reflects the company's massive R&D investment and architectural advantages. The tight integration between hardware and software allows Apple to extract maximum performance per watt, a metric where it consistently leads the industry.

What This Means for Businesses

Small businesses and professionals evaluating their next laptop purchase should strongly consider the MacBook Air M5, particularly if their workflow involves standard productivity tasks, creative work, or software development. The combination of performance, portability, and battery life makes it suitable for virtually any mobile work scenario.

For organisations standardising on Windows, the MacBook Air's success should prompt evaluation of whether current Windows device selections offer comparable value. Pairing capable hardware with properly licensed software — such as a genuine Windows 11 key — ensures businesses get the most from their technology investments regardless of platform choice.

Key Takeaways

  • M5 chip delivers meaningful performance gains with improved CPU, GPU, and Neural Engine capabilities
  • Faster storage and maintained exceptional battery life enhance everyday usability
  • Apple's iterative improvement strategy proves more effective than dramatic annual redesigns
  • The Neural Engine upgrade supports growing on-device AI processing demands
  • Windows ultraportable competitors face an increasingly difficult competitive landscape

Looking Ahead

The MacBook Air M5 solidifies Apple's position at the top of the ultraportable market, but the real story may be what comes next. As AI workloads grow more demanding and Apple Intelligence features expand, future MacBook Air models may need to balance the fanless design ethos with the thermal requirements of more powerful AI processing. For now, the M5 Air represents the optimal balance point — and an excellent time to buy for anyone due for a laptop upgrade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the MacBook Air M5 worth upgrading from M4?

Most reviewers suggest the upgrade is modest for M4 owners, with the biggest improvements in storage speed and Neural Engine performance. Users on M3 or older chips will see much more significant gains across the board.

Can the MacBook Air M5 handle professional workloads?

Yes, the M5 chip makes the MacBook Air capable of handling most professional workflows including document editing, video conferencing, light video editing, software development, and AI-powered productivity features.

How does the MacBook Air M5 compare to Windows ultraportables?

The MacBook Air M5 leads in power efficiency, battery life, and software optimisation. While Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite Windows laptops have narrowed the gap in raw performance, Apple's vertical integration continues to deliver advantages in real-world usage.
