Wiim Sound Smart Speaker Review: The HomePod Successor Apple Never Made

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-23

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Wiim Sound smart speaker launches with premium audio, touchscreen interface, and cross-platform support
  • Reviewers call it the HomePod successor Apple fans have been waiting for
  • Supports AirPlay 2, Google Cast, and Spotify Connect for ecosystem-agnostic operation
  • Highlights growing market opportunity for specialist audio companies in smart speakers

What Happened

Wiim has launched the Wiim Sound, a touchscreen-equipped smart speaker that reviewers are calling the HomePod successor that Apple fans have been waiting for. The device features a tasteful industrial design reminiscent of premium audio equipment, paired with an immersive listening experience that positions it as a serious alternative in a smart speaker market that has been dominated by compromises between audio quality and smart functionality.

The Wiim Sound distinguishes itself from competitors through its integrated touchscreen interface, which provides direct control over music playback, streaming services, and smart home devices without requiring voice commands or smartphone interaction. This design choice addresses a persistent complaint about smart speakers: the frustration of voice-only interfaces that fail in noisy environments, during phone calls, or when users simply prefer tactile control.

Early reviews have praised the speaker's audio performance as exceptional for its price point, with sound quality that approaches or matches speakers costing significantly more. The device supports multiple streaming protocols, including AirPlay 2, Google Cast, and Spotify Connect, making it platform-agnostic — a critical differentiator for households with mixed Apple and Android ecosystems.

Background and Context

The smart speaker market has undergone significant fragmentation since the category's early days, when Amazon Echo and Google Home competed primarily on voice assistant capabilities. As consumers discovered that voice assistants plateaued in usefulness faster than expected, the market bifurcated into two segments: budget speakers focused on utility and premium speakers focused on audio quality.

Apple's HomePod line has occupied the premium audio segment but with significant limitations — high pricing, exclusive ecosystem integration, and a reliance on Siri that many users find less capable than competing assistants. The original HomePod was discontinued in 2021 before being relaunched in 2023, and the HomePod Mini serves a different market segment focused on compact size rather than room-filling sound.

Wiim, originally known for its streaming audio components and hi-fi adapters, has built a reputation in audiophile circles for delivering exceptional sound quality at accessible price points. The Wiim Sound represents the company's most ambitious consumer product, expanding from audio components into a full smart speaker that competes directly with established brands. For users who manage their digital lives through enterprise productivity software and multiple connected devices, a platform-agnostic smart speaker that works with both Apple and Google ecosystems eliminates a persistent friction point in mixed-device households.

Why This Matters

The Wiim Sound's positive reception highlights a growing market opportunity for smart speakers that prioritize audio quality and cross-platform compatibility over proprietary ecosystem lock-in. Apple's HomePod line requires commitment to the Apple ecosystem for full functionality, Amazon's Echo speakers prioritize Alexa integration over audio fidelity, and Google's Nest speakers fall somewhere in between. The Wiim Sound's platform-agnostic approach — supporting AirPlay 2, Google Cast, and Spotify Connect simultaneously — represents a different philosophy that may resonate with the large segment of consumers who use products from multiple technology ecosystems.

The touchscreen interface is equally significant from a product design perspective. As smart speaker voice assistants have plateaued in capability and consumer enthusiasm for voice interaction has moderated, tactile interfaces provide an alternative interaction model that feels more familiar and reliable. The success of Amazon's Echo Show line — which added screens to Alexa speakers — demonstrated consumer demand for visual and touch interfaces on smart speakers, but Amazon's implementation prioritized shopping and entertainment integration over pure audio quality.

For the audio industry, the Wiim Sound represents the maturation of a new category: the audiophile smart speaker. Previous attempts to combine high-fidelity audio with smart capabilities — including Harman Kardon's Citation series and Bowers & Wilkins' Formation line — were priced at levels that limited their market appeal. Wiim's ability to deliver comparable audio quality at a more accessible price point could expand the addressable market for premium connected speakers significantly.

Industry Impact

The smart speaker market, valued at approximately $12 billion globally, faces a crossroads. Growth has slowed from the explosive adoption rates of 2018-2021, and the major platform players — Amazon, Google, and Apple — have all reduced their investment in new smart speaker hardware. This retrenchment has created an opening for specialist audio companies like Wiim, Sonos, and Bose to capture market share with products that emphasize audio quality over smart assistant integration.

For Apple specifically, the Wiim Sound highlights a gap in its product lineup. The HomePod, while well-regarded for audio quality, is expensive and limited to the Apple ecosystem. The HomePod Mini is affordable but compromises on sound quality. A mid-range, touchscreen-equipped Apple speaker that matches the Wiim Sound's capabilities would be a natural addition to the Apple product line, and Wiim's success may accelerate Apple's development timeline for such a product.

Smart home platform providers are also paying attention. The Wiim Sound's Matter and Thread support ensures compatibility with the emerging smart home standard that Apple, Google, Amazon, and Samsung have all committed to supporting. As the smart home ecosystem consolidates around Matter, speakers that support the standard natively will have a significant advantage over those tied to proprietary platforms. Professionals who use affordable Microsoft Office licence products for work and manage their smart homes from the same devices appreciate the simplicity of platform-agnostic accessories.

Expert Perspective

Audio engineers note that the Wiim Sound benefits from advances in digital signal processing and speaker driver technology that have made premium audio performance achievable at lower price points. The miniaturization of high-quality audio components and the sophistication of room correction algorithms allow smaller speakers to produce sound that was previously only achievable with larger, more expensive hardware.

Smart home analysts emphasize that the touchscreen interface addresses a real usability gap in the smart speaker market. Voice interfaces, while convenient for simple commands, become frustrating for more complex interactions like browsing music libraries, managing multi-room audio, or controlling smart home scenes with multiple variables. The touchscreen provides a visual, interactive interface that bridges this usability gap without abandoning voice control entirely.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses outfitting conference rooms, common areas, or reception spaces with background music and smart home capabilities, the Wiim Sound's combination of audio quality, cross-platform compatibility, and touchscreen interface makes it a practical choice. The device's support for multiple streaming protocols ensures compatibility with whatever music service the organization uses, and the touchscreen allows any employee to control playback without needing to download a specific app or interact with a voice assistant.

Home office professionals will also find value in a premium smart speaker that integrates seamlessly with both Apple and Android devices. As remote work continues to be a significant portion of the workforce, the quality of home office environments — including audio for both work calls and ambient music — directly impacts productivity and satisfaction. Pairing quality audio hardware with a properly set up workstation running genuine Windows 11 key creates a professional home office environment.

Looking Ahead

The Wiim Sound's market reception will be closely watched by both established smart speaker manufacturers and specialist audio companies considering entering the connected speaker market. If Wiim can translate positive reviews into sales volume, it could establish a new product category — the audiophile smart speaker — that pressures Apple, Amazon, and Google to improve audio quality in their own offerings. The broader trend of touchscreen integration and platform-agnostic design is likely to influence the next generation of smart speakers across the industry.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes the Wiim Sound different from the HomePod?

The Wiim Sound offers cross-platform compatibility (AirPlay 2, Google Cast, Spotify Connect), a touchscreen interface, and competitive audio quality at a more accessible price point, while the HomePod is limited to the Apple ecosystem.

Does the Wiim Sound work with both iPhone and Android?

Yes, the Wiim Sound supports AirPlay 2 for Apple devices, Google Cast for Android devices, and Spotify Connect for both platforms, making it fully cross-platform compatible.

Is the Wiim Sound good for home offices?

Yes, its premium audio quality, platform-agnostic streaming support, and touchscreen control make it a practical choice for home offices where users need quality sound for both work and leisure.
