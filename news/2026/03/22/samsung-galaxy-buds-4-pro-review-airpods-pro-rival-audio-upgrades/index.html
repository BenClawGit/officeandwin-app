Samsung Galaxy Buds 4 Pro Take the Fight to AirPods Pro with Refined Audio and Head Gesture Controls

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-22

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Samsung Galaxy Buds 4 Pro feature a new dual-driver system with planar magnetic tweeter for superior audio
  • Head gesture controls enable hands-free call management and media playback
  • Galaxy AI integration brings live translation and 360 spatial audio capabilities
  • Reviewers consider them the best Samsung earbuds yet and genuine AirPods Pro competitors

Samsung's Latest Earbuds Deliver Meaningful Upgrades in a Familiar Package

Samsung has unveiled the Galaxy Buds 4 Pro, the company's latest premium wireless earbuds that arrive with a refined design, upgraded acoustic components, and the introduction of head gesture controls. While the Buds 4 Pro may not revolutionise the wireless earbud category, early reviews suggest they represent the best Samsung earbuds to date — and genuine competitors to Apple's AirPods Pro in several key areas including sound quality, noise cancellation, and ecosystem integration.

The most significant hardware upgrade comes in the acoustic department. Samsung has equipped the Buds 4 Pro with a two-way driver design featuring a 10.5mm dynamic driver paired with a 6.1mm planar magnetic tweeter. This combination produces what reviewers describe as a wider, more airy soundstage compared to the predecessor Galaxy Buds 3 Pro. The dual-driver approach allows for cleaner frequency separation, with the dynamic driver handling bass and midrange while the planar magnetic tweeter delivers precise, detailed highs.

Head gesture controls represent the most visible new feature, allowing users to answer or reject calls, control media playback, and interact with notifications through subtle head movements. The feature uses the earbuds' built-in motion sensors to detect nods and shakes, offering a hands-free control method that's particularly useful during exercise, cooking, or other situations where reaching for the earbuds or a phone isn't convenient.

Background and Context

The wireless earbuds market has matured significantly since Apple's original AirPods essentially created the category in 2016. What was once a simple wireless listening experience has evolved into a sophisticated platform for audio processing, noise cancellation, health monitoring, and AI-powered features. Samsung has been one of Apple's most consistent competitors in this space, iterating through multiple generations of Galaxy Buds with increasingly competitive offerings.

The Galaxy Buds 3 Pro from 2024 were widely regarded as Samsung's strongest challenge to the AirPods Pro, but they fell short in certain areas including noise cancellation consistency and ecosystem integration depth. The Buds 4 Pro address many of these criticisms while adding new capabilities that push the category forward. Samsung's Galaxy AI integration, which brings features like live translation and contextual awareness to the earbuds, represents the kind of platform-level differentiation that distinguishes premium earbuds from commodity wireless headphones.

The return of 360 audio support, which creates a spatial sound experience using head tracking, aligns the Buds 4 Pro with Apple's Spatial Audio and similar technologies from Sony and other competitors. This feature transforms standard stereo content into an immersive listening experience, particularly effective for movie and TV content on compatible devices.

Why This Matters

The wireless earbuds market is increasingly a proxy war for broader ecosystem competition between Samsung, Apple, and Google. Earbuds serve as a gateway device — once a user invests in premium earbuds that integrate deeply with a particular ecosystem, they're more likely to stay within that ecosystem for their phone, tablet, and laptop purchases. Samsung's ability to offer genuinely competitive earbuds matters not just for the accessory market but for the company's broader device strategy.

The audio upgrades in the Buds 4 Pro are particularly noteworthy because they represent a meaningful technical advancement rather than incremental improvement. Planar magnetic drivers, previously found primarily in high-end over-ear headphones, deliver a level of detail and clarity that traditional dynamic drivers struggle to match. Samsung's decision to incorporate this technology into earbuds at a consumer price point democratises access to audiophile-grade sound quality. For professionals who spend hours in virtual meetings using enterprise productivity software, clear audio reproduction directly impacts communication quality.

The head gesture controls, while potentially gimmicky in isolation, reflect a broader trend toward more natural, intuitive interfaces for wearable technology. As AI capabilities expand — Samsung's Galaxy AI and Apple's Siri are both pushing toward more proactive, context-aware assistance — having hands-free interaction methods becomes increasingly important for making these features practical rather than theoretical.

Industry Impact

Samsung's Buds 4 Pro arrive in an increasingly crowded premium earbuds market. Beyond Apple's AirPods Pro, competition now includes Google's Pixel Buds Pro, Sony's WF-1000XM series, Bose's QuietComfort Ultra Earbuds, and numerous offerings from brands like Sennheiser and Jabra. The differentiation challenge is real — once noise cancellation, sound quality, and battery life reach a certain threshold, brands must find other ways to justify premium pricing.

Samsung's answer is ecosystem integration and AI features. Galaxy AI capabilities like live translation — where the earbuds can translate spoken language in real time — move beyond traditional audio features into genuine utility that competitors struggle to match without similar ecosystem depth. These features work best within the Galaxy ecosystem, creating the kind of stickiness that drives brand loyalty across device categories.

The pricing dynamics of the premium earbuds market also reflect broader trends in consumer technology. As smartphones plateau in terms of groundbreaking innovation, accessories and wearables have become increasingly important revenue streams and ecosystem lock-in mechanisms for major technology companies. Premium earbuds that cost two hundred dollars or more represent significant consumer investment, making product quality and reliability essential for brand trust.

Expert Perspective

Audio technology reviewers note that the Galaxy Buds 4 Pro's dual-driver system represents a genuine technical achievement in the earbuds form factor. The challenge of fitting a planar magnetic driver alongside a traditional dynamic driver in something that weighs a few grams and fits inside an ear canal requires significant engineering precision. Samsung's success here suggests the company is serious about competing on audio quality, not just features and ecosystem integration.

The head gesture control implementation receives praise for its subtlety — the system requires deliberate movements to activate, reducing false triggers from normal head motion. This is a lesson learned from early implementations of gesture controls in other devices, where sensitivity issues led to frustrating false activations that undermined the feature's utility.

What This Means for Businesses

For business users, the Galaxy Buds 4 Pro's improvements in call quality, noise cancellation, and AI-powered features make them viable tools for professional communication. The live translation feature, in particular, has obvious applications for international business interactions. Companies with globally distributed teams could find the real-time translation capability a genuine productivity enhancer during multilingual meetings.

IT departments evaluating approved accessories for corporate device programs should consider the Buds 4 Pro's compatibility with both Samsung and non-Samsung Android devices, as well as basic functionality with iPhones. For organisations standardised on Microsoft's productivity stack with an affordable Microsoft Office licence, the earbuds integrate well with Teams and other communication platforms on both mobile and desktop, including systems running a genuine Windows 11 key.

Key Takeaways

  • Samsung Galaxy Buds 4 Pro feature a new dual-driver system with a planar magnetic tweeter for improved audio quality
  • Head gesture controls allow hands-free interaction for calls, media, and notifications
  • Galaxy AI integration brings live translation and contextual awareness features
  • 360 audio with head tracking returns, creating an immersive spatial sound experience
  • The earbuds are considered genuine AirPods Pro competitors, particularly within the Galaxy ecosystem
  • Upgrading from Galaxy Buds 3 Pro is optional but worthwhile for audio enthusiasts

Looking Ahead

Samsung is expected to continue pushing Galaxy AI capabilities across its earbuds lineup, with potential future features including real-time conversation summarisation and proactive notifications based on environmental context. The Buds 4 Pro set a new benchmark for Samsung's audio engineering, and the question now is whether the next generation of AirPods Pro will respond with similarly ambitious upgrades. The wireless earbuds arms race shows no signs of slowing down.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Galaxy Buds 4 Pro better than AirPods Pro?

Reviewers note the Galaxy Buds 4 Pro are better in several areas including audio quality thanks to the dual-driver system, and they offer unique features like head gesture controls and Galaxy AI live translation. However, AirPods Pro still have advantages in Apple ecosystem integration.

What's new in the Galaxy Buds 4 Pro vs Buds 3 Pro?

Key upgrades include a dual-driver acoustic system with planar magnetic tweeter, new head gesture controls, improved noise cancellation, enhanced durability, the return of 360 audio, and deeper Galaxy AI integration including live translation.

Do Galaxy Buds 4 Pro work with iPhone?

The Galaxy Buds 4 Pro provide basic functionality with iPhones including audio playback and call handling, but advanced features like Galaxy AI integration, 360 audio, and head gesture customisation require a Samsung Galaxy device for full functionality.
