ChatGPT Ads Hit $100 Million Annualised Revenue in Just Six Weeks as OpenAI Expands Advertising Push

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-28

⚡ Quick Summary

  • OpenAI's ChatGPT advertising pilot crossed $100 million in annualised revenue within six weeks of launch
  • Less than 20% of eligible users are currently shown ads daily, suggesting massive growth potential
  • The company has expanded to over 600 advertisers with self-serve capabilities launching in April
  • Global expansion to Australia, New Zealand, and Canada is planned in the coming weeks

What Happened

OpenAI's nascent advertising business has achieved a remarkable early milestone: the ChatGPT ads pilot in the United States crossed $100 million in annualised revenue within just six weeks of its launch. The company disclosed the figure on March 27, 2026, pointing to robust early demand from advertisers eager to reach ChatGPT's massive and highly engaged user base.

The numbers are particularly striking given how conservatively OpenAI has deployed advertising so far. While approximately 85% of ChatGPT users are currently eligible to see ads, fewer than 20% are actually shown ads on any given day. This deliberate restraint suggests that OpenAI is prioritising user experience over immediate revenue maximisation — a strategy that leaves enormous room for growth as the company refines its ad targeting and expands its advertiser base.

OpenAI has now grown its advertiser roster to over 600 companies, with nearly 80% of small and medium-sized businesses expressing interest in ChatGPT advertising. The company plans to launch self-serve advertiser capabilities in April 2026, which will dramatically lower the barrier to entry for businesses wanting to reach ChatGPT's audience. Global expansion to Australia, New Zealand, and Canada is expected in the coming weeks.

Background and Context

The arrival of advertising on ChatGPT represents a notable philosophical shift for OpenAI. CEO Sam Altman had long resisted the idea, famously describing ads in 2024 as a last resort business model. Yet the economics of running one of the most compute-intensive services in technology history eventually demanded diversification beyond subscription fees. OpenAI's annualised revenue crossed $20 billion in 2025, but the company's infrastructure costs — particularly for GPU compute — continued to outpace revenue growth.

The decision to introduce ads was announced in January 2026 and the pilot launched shortly after. The approach mirrors the trajectory of other platforms that initially resisted advertising before recognising its potential. Google's founding team was famously sceptical of ads before building the world's most profitable advertising business. Meta's early years saw similar resistance before ads became the backbone of its revenue model.

What makes ChatGPT advertising particularly interesting is the context in which ads appear. Unlike traditional search or social media advertising, ChatGPT ads are served within conversational interactions where users are actively seeking information, making purchasing decisions, or solving problems. This creates an advertising context that is potentially more valuable than almost any other digital medium, because the intent signals are extraordinarily clear.

Why This Matters

The $100 million annualised run rate in six weeks is not just a vanity metric — it validates an entirely new advertising paradigm. If ChatGPT can successfully monetise conversational AI interactions through advertising, it establishes a template that every other AI chatbot and assistant will attempt to replicate. This could fundamentally reshape the digital advertising landscape by redirecting spend away from traditional search and social platforms toward conversational AI interfaces.

For the broader AI industry, OpenAI's advertising success addresses one of the most persistent questions hanging over the sector: how will AI companies make money? While subscription models have shown promise, advertising offers a path to monetising the vast majority of users who will never pay for a subscription. With self-serve capabilities launching in April, even small businesses selling enterprise productivity software or other digital products could potentially reach ChatGPT's audience through targeted ad placements.

Industry Impact

The implications for Google are particularly significant. Google's search advertising business, which generates hundreds of billions in annual revenue, faces its most credible threat in two decades. If users increasingly turn to ChatGPT and similar AI assistants for information queries — and if advertisers follow the eyeballs — Google's core business model could face meaningful erosion over time.

For the advertising industry more broadly, ChatGPT ads represent the first major new premium advertising surface to emerge since TikTok. The conversational context provides unique targeting opportunities because advertisers can reach users at precise moments of intent. A user asking ChatGPT about office productivity software, for example, could be shown relevant ads for affordable Microsoft Office licences at the exact moment of peak purchase intent.

The planned global expansion also signals that OpenAI sees advertising as a permanent and significant revenue stream, not merely an experiment. The rollout to Australia, New Zealand, and Canada suggests a methodical approach to international expansion, likely prioritising English-speaking markets before tackling the complexities of multilingual ad serving.

Expert Perspective

Digital advertising analysts have noted that OpenAI's early metrics are remarkably strong for a new advertising platform. The combination of high user engagement, clear intent signals, and low ad dismissal rates creates conditions that are extremely attractive to advertisers. The fact that nearly 80% of small and medium-sized businesses have expressed interest before self-serve tools are even available suggests pent-up demand that could drive rapid revenue acceleration once those tools launch.

However, the long-term success of ChatGPT advertising will depend on OpenAI's ability to maintain user trust while increasing ad load. The current approach of showing ads to fewer than 20% of eligible users daily is sustainable but may face pressure as investors push for revenue growth. Finding the right balance will be critical — too aggressive and users will seek alternatives; too conservative and the platform will underperform its revenue potential.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses considering digital advertising strategies, ChatGPT ads represent a channel that deserves immediate attention. The April launch of self-serve capabilities will make it accessible to businesses of all sizes. Early adopters may benefit from lower competition and cost-per-click rates before the platform becomes saturated. Companies selling digital products like genuine Windows 11 keys or software licences could find the conversational context particularly valuable for reaching high-intent buyers.

Businesses should also consider the defensive implications. If ChatGPT advertising proves effective, it may draw budget away from existing channels like Google Ads and Meta Ads. Marketing teams should begin planning how to allocate budgets across this emerging channel while monitoring early performance data as it becomes available after the self-serve launch.

Key Takeaways

  • ChatGPT ads reached $100 million annualised revenue in just six weeks
  • Only 20% of eligible users see ads daily, indicating massive untapped potential
  • Over 600 advertisers are already participating with 80% SMB interest
  • Self-serve ad tools launching April 2026 will dramatically expand access
  • Global expansion to Australia, New Zealand, and Canada imminent
  • OpenAI reports no negative impact on user trust metrics
  • The move represents a philosophical shift from Sam Altman's earlier anti-advertising stance

Looking Ahead

The next critical milestone will be the April launch of self-serve advertising capabilities and the initial results from international expansion. If OpenAI can maintain its early momentum while preserving user satisfaction, ChatGPT advertising could become one of the most significant new revenue streams in the technology industry. Advertisers, competitors, and investors alike will be watching closely as the platform scales beyond its pilot phase into full commercial operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much revenue are ChatGPT ads generating?

OpenAI's ChatGPT advertising pilot has crossed $100 million in annualised revenue within just six weeks of its US launch. This figure represents the revenue run rate based on current ad performance, with significant room to grow as less than 20% of eligible users are currently shown ads on any given day.

Will ChatGPT ads be available outside the United States?

Yes. OpenAI has confirmed plans to expand ChatGPT advertising to additional countries in the coming weeks, including Australia, New Zealand, and Canada. The global rollout will be accompanied by the launch of self-serve advertiser capabilities in April 2026, making it easier for businesses of all sizes to place ads on the platform.

Are ChatGPT ads affecting user experience?

According to OpenAI, the company is seeing no impact on consumer trust metrics and reports low dismissal rates for ads. The company says ad relevance is improving continuously as it learns from user feedback. Currently, about 85% of ChatGPT users are eligible to see ads, but the company is being selective about ad frequency.
