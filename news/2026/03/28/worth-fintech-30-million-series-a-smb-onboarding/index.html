Worth Raises $30 Million to Automate SMB Onboarding for Banks and Financial Services

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-28

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Worth raises $30 million Series A to automate SMB onboarding for financial institutions
  • Total funding reaches $55 million following a $25 million seed round
  • Manual business onboarding costs banks $500-$2,000 per application, limiting service to smaller businesses
  • Platform automates identity verification, financial analysis, and compliance for 33 million U.S. small businesses

What Happened

Worth, a fintech startup focused on helping financial services companies onboard and underwrite small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs), has raised a $30 million Series A led by Fulcrum Equity Partners. The round follows a $25 million seed, bringing the company's total funding to $55 million — a substantial war chest for a company attacking one of financial services' most persistent pain points: the costly, time-consuming process of evaluating and onboarding business customers.

Worth's platform automates the identity verification, financial analysis, and risk assessment processes that banks, lenders, and financial institutions traditionally handle through manual review. For SMBs applying for business accounts, loans, or financial products, this translates to faster approvals and less paperwork. For financial institutions, it means lower customer acquisition costs, reduced fraud risk, and the ability to profitably serve smaller business customers that would otherwise be uneconomical to underwrite manually.

The company targets a specific gap in financial services infrastructure: while consumer onboarding and underwriting has been heavily automated through companies like Plaid, Alloy, and Socure, business onboarding remains largely manual. Business verification is inherently more complex than consumer verification — it involves corporate registrations, beneficial ownership identification, financial statement analysis, industry risk assessment, and compliance checks across multiple regulatory frameworks.

Background and Context

The SMB financial services market represents an enormous and underserved opportunity. There are approximately 33 million small businesses in the United States alone, generating over $12 trillion in annual revenue. Yet these businesses consistently report that accessing financial services — bank accounts, credit lines, insurance, payment processing — is more difficult, expensive, and time-consuming than it should be. The root cause is operational: manually evaluating a business customer can cost financial institutions $500-$2,000 per application, making smaller accounts unprofitable to serve.

The regulatory landscape has made business onboarding even more complex in recent years. Anti-money laundering (AML) regulations, Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements, and the Corporate Transparency Act's beneficial ownership reporting mandates have added layers of compliance checks that financial institutions must perform before opening business accounts. These requirements are important for preventing financial crime, but they've also increased the friction that legitimate businesses face when trying to access financial services.

Several companies have attempted to address pieces of this puzzle — Middesk focuses on business identity verification, Codat on accounting data aggregation, and Ocrolus on document analysis — but Worth appears to be building a more comprehensive platform that handles the entire onboarding and underwriting workflow from initial application to risk decision.

Why This Matters

Financial inclusion for small businesses has macroeconomic significance. When businesses can't efficiently access banking, credit, and financial services, economic growth is constrained. The manual, expensive nature of SMB onboarding creates a systemic bias toward larger businesses that generate enough revenue to justify the onboarding cost, while smaller businesses — which collectively represent the backbone of employment and innovation — face friction that limits their growth potential.

Worth's platform could help level this playing field by reducing the per-customer cost of business onboarding to a point where financial institutions can profitably serve smaller businesses. This has implications not just for the businesses themselves but for the broader economy: when small businesses can more easily access credit, they invest in growth, hire employees, and contribute to economic activity. Companies selling enterprise productivity software and business tools to SMBs would benefit from a healthier small business ecosystem with better access to growth capital.

Industry Impact

For established banks and credit unions, Worth's platform represents an opportunity to compete more effectively with fintech challengers that have built streamlined digital onboarding experiences. Traditional financial institutions have struggled to digitize business onboarding because the process involves integrating data from multiple sources — state registrations, credit bureaus, financial statements, beneficial ownership databases — each with its own format, API, and reliability characteristics. A platform that handles this integration reduces the technology investment required for banks to offer competitive digital experiences.

Fintech lenders and neobanks should view Worth as both a potential partner and a competitive enabler. Companies like Mercury, Brex, and Bluevine have differentiated themselves partly through faster business account opening, but as platforms like Worth make similar capabilities available to traditional banks, this differentiation erodes. The competitive battleground shifts from onboarding speed to the quality and pricing of the financial products themselves.

For the regulatory technology (RegTech) sector, Worth's raise signals continued investor appetite for compliance automation solutions. The regulatory burden on financial institutions continues to grow, and manual compliance processes are both expensive and error-prone. Automated platforms that can maintain compliance while reducing costs address a structural need that won't diminish regardless of economic cycles.

Expert Perspective

Worth's $55 million total funding for a business onboarding platform reflects the size of the opportunity and the difficulty of the problem. Business underwriting is fundamentally harder than consumer underwriting because businesses are more heterogeneous — a restaurant, a software company, and a construction firm have completely different risk profiles, financial structures, and data availability. Building models that can accurately assess risk across this diversity requires substantial data acquisition, model development, and industry-specific expertise.

The $25 million seed followed by a $30 million Series A suggests strong early traction — investors typically don't lead Series A rounds at this scale without evidence of product-market fit and meaningful customer revenue. Fulcrum Equity Partners' lead position indicates confidence in Worth's ability to scale its platform across the financial services industry.

What This Means for Businesses

For SMB owners, platforms like Worth should eventually translate to faster, less frustrating experiences when applying for business bank accounts, loans, and financial products. The reduction in manual processing means faster decisions, and the automation of compliance checks means fewer back-and-forth documentation requests. Business owners who need to focus on running their operations — including managing their technology stack with genuine Windows 11 key systems and affordable Microsoft Office licence deployments — benefit from financial onboarding that doesn't consume days of administrative effort.

For financial institutions evaluating their SMB strategy, Worth's funding validates the business case for automated onboarding investment. Institutions that continue relying on manual processes will find themselves at an increasing competitive disadvantage as automated platforms lower the cost of customer acquisition for early adopters.

  • 33 million U.S. small businesses represent an underserved market for financial services

Looking Ahead

Worth's next milestones will likely include expanding its financial institution customer base, building out industry-specific underwriting models, and potentially expanding internationally where SMB financial access challenges are even more acute. The broader trend toward automated business onboarding is irreversible — the combination of growing regulatory requirements and competitive pressure from digital-first financial institutions ensures that automation adoption will continue to accelerate. Worth's position as an infrastructure provider in this space gives it leverage that grows with every new financial institution customer.
