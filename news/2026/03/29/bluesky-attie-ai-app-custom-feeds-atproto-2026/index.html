Bluesky Launches Attie: An AI-Powered App That Lets Anyone Build Custom Social Feeds Without Code

By OfficeandWin Tech Desk · 2026-03-29

⚡ Quick Summary

  • Bluesky unveiled Attie, a standalone AI app built on Anthropic Claude that lets users create custom feeds using plain language
  • The app is the first product from Jay Graber's innovation team since stepping down as Bluesky CEO
  • Attie leverages the open AT Protocol to instantly personalize without weeks of learning
  • Future plans include vibe-coding features that could turn the AT Protocol into a no-code app platform

What Happened

Bluesky, the decentralized social network that has positioned itself as the open alternative to X (formerly Twitter), has unveiled a standalone AI-powered application called Attie that allows users to build custom algorithmic feeds using nothing more than natural language prompts. The app was presented for the first time at the Atmosphere conference by Bluesky co-founder Jay Graber, who recently stepped down as CEO to become chief innovation officer, and CTO Paul Frazee.

Attie represents Bluesky's most ambitious product launch since the social network itself. Built on Anthropic's Claude AI model, the app leverages the open AT Protocol (atproto) to give users unprecedented control over their content discovery experience. Users sign in with their existing Atmosphere credentials and can immediately begin creating personalized feeds by describing what they want to see in plain English.

The app is the first product built by Graber's new innovation team since her leadership transition, and conference attendees at Atmosphere are serving as the initial beta testing cohort. Interim CEO Toni Schneider, who also serves as a partner at Bluesky backer True Ventures, emphasized that Attie is a completely standalone product — not a feature grafted onto the existing Bluesky app.

Background and Context

The social media landscape has been grappling with the algorithm problem for years. Users have long complained that opaque, engagement-maximizing algorithms show them content designed to keep them scrolling rather than content they actually want to see. Bluesky's original approach to this problem was custom feeds — user-created algorithmic filters that anyone could build and share. However, creating these feeds required technical knowledge, limiting their adoption to developers and power users.

Attie eliminates this barrier entirely. By wrapping feed creation in a conversational AI interface, Bluesky is effectively democratizing algorithmic curation. A user who wants to see only posts about vintage photography from people they follow, or breaking news about artificial intelligence without promotional content, can simply describe that preference and Attie will construct the corresponding feed.

The choice of Anthropic's Claude as the underlying AI engine is strategically significant. While competitors like Meta and X have built their AI features on proprietary models, Bluesky's decision to partner with an independent AI lab reinforces its commitment to an open ecosystem. Claude's reputation for nuanced language understanding and safety-first design aligns with Bluesky's stated goal of building AI products that are "people-focused" rather than engagement-focused.

The AT Protocol's open architecture makes Attie possible in ways that would be difficult on closed platforms. Because data on atproto is portable and transparent, Attie can immediately understand a user's interests, interactions, and social graph without requiring weeks of learning — a significant advantage over traditional AI personalization that relies on gradual data accumulation.

Why This Matters

Attie's launch represents a philosophical inflection point in how social media platforms approach artificial intelligence. While Meta, X, and TikTok have deployed AI primarily to maximize engagement metrics and advertising revenue, Bluesky is using AI to give users more control — a fundamentally different value proposition that could reshape user expectations across the industry.

The timing is particularly relevant as regulatory pressure mounts globally against opaque algorithmic systems. The European Union's Digital Services Act already requires platforms to offer non-algorithmic feed options, and similar legislation is advancing in Australia, the UK, and several US states. Attie's model of transparent, user-directed algorithmic curation could become a template for regulatory compliance that other platforms will be forced to emulate.

For the broader AI industry, Attie demonstrates a compelling use case that goes beyond the chatbot paradigm. Rather than replacing human activity, the AI serves as an intermediary that translates human intent into technical configuration — a pattern that could be applied to everything from email filtering to enterprise productivity software customization. The implications for workplace tools are substantial: imagine describing your ideal email triage system or document organization scheme in plain language and having AI implement it instantly.

Schneider's comment that Attie is "the beginning of just having a lot more people be able to build on top of the Atmosphere" hints at even larger ambitions. If Attie evolves to support "vibe-coding" — building simple applications through conversational prompts — it could lower the barrier to app development on the AT Protocol to near zero, potentially creating an explosion of niche social experiences.

Industry Impact

The competitive implications for established social media platforms are significant. Meta has invested billions in AI features across Instagram and Facebook, but those features primarily serve Meta's advertising business by improving content recommendations and ad targeting. X has integrated Grok, its AI chatbot, but primarily as a conversational tool rather than a feed customization engine. Neither platform has given users the ability to design their own algorithms.

For developers building on the AT Protocol, Attie represents both opportunity and validation. The app demonstrates that the protocol's open data architecture enables AI-powered experiences that closed platforms cannot easily replicate. This could accelerate developer migration to the AT Protocol ecosystem, creating a virtuous cycle of more apps, more users, and more data that further enhances AI capabilities.

The advertising industry should also take note. User-directed algorithmic feeds could fundamentally alter how brand content is discovered and consumed on social platforms. If users can explicitly filter out promotional content or specify exactly what types of brand communications they want to see, traditional social media advertising models may need to evolve toward more value-driven content strategies.

Privacy advocates will likely view Attie favorably, as it puts algorithmic control in users' hands rather than platform operators'. However, the use of Anthropic's Claude means user prompts and feed preferences are processed by a third-party AI system, raising questions about data sharing between Bluesky and Anthropic that will need to be addressed transparently.

Expert Perspective

The integration of conversational AI with social feed curation addresses a long-standing usability problem in content personalization. Traditional recommendation systems learn from implicit signals — what you click, how long you linger, what you scroll past — which often leads to filter bubbles and engagement traps. Attie's explicit, language-based approach allows users to articulate preferences that implicit signals cannot capture, such as "show me perspectives I disagree with" or "prioritize educational content over entertainment."

This represents a meaningful advance in human-AI interaction design. Rather than asking users to understand technical concepts like keyword filters, boolean operators, or recommendation weights, Attie translates natural language intent into sophisticated feed configurations. The AI acts as a universal translator between human desire and technical implementation — a pattern that, if successful, could be adopted across countless software categories where configuration complexity limits user adoption.

The decision to launch as a standalone app rather than an integrated feature also signals strategic maturity. By keeping Attie separate from the core Bluesky experience, the team can iterate rapidly without risking the stability of their primary social network while simultaneously expanding the AT Protocol ecosystem beyond a single application.

What This Means for Businesses

For businesses with a social media presence, Attie's launch signals a shift toward audience-controlled content discovery that should inform content strategy immediately. When users can explicitly define what types of content they want to see, the brands that succeed will be those producing genuinely valuable content rather than relying on algorithmic amplification through engagement bait.

Companies investing in digital transformation should watch the AT Protocol ecosystem closely. The ability for users to port their data, preferences, and social connections across applications represents a fundamentally different model from the walled gardens that dominate today's enterprise and consumer software landscapes. Organizations running affordable Microsoft Office licence deployments should consider how open protocol approaches might eventually influence productivity software as well.

Marketing teams should begin experimenting with Bluesky and the AT Protocol now, before the ecosystem reaches mainstream scale. Early movers on emerging platforms consistently benefit from lower competition and stronger organic reach. The brands that establish presence and credibility on Bluesky today will be best positioned when AI-curated feeds make authentic, high-quality content the primary driver of visibility.

Looking Ahead

Attie's beta launch at Atmosphere is just the opening move in what appears to be a broader strategy to make the AT Protocol the foundation for AI-native social experiences. If the app successfully demonstrates that users prefer explicit algorithmic control over opaque recommendation systems, it could accelerate regulatory and competitive pressure on established platforms to offer similar capabilities. Watch for Attie's public beta timeline, developer API announcements, and whether competing platforms respond with their own user-directed feed customization tools. The vibe-coding features teased by the team could be the most transformative development — if realized, they would make the AT Protocol not just a social network but a no-code application platform with AI at its core.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bluesky Attie?

Attie is a standalone AI-powered app built by Bluesky's innovation team that allows users to create custom social media feeds by describing what they want to see in natural language. It runs on Anthropic's Claude AI and uses the open AT Protocol to understand user preferences instantly.

How does Attie differ from regular social media algorithms?

Unlike traditional algorithms that optimize for engagement metrics using implicit signals like clicks and scroll time, Attie lets users explicitly describe their content preferences in plain language. This gives users direct control over what they see rather than relying on opaque recommendation systems.

When will Attie be available to the public?

Attie was first presented at the Atmosphere conference in late March 2026, with conference attendees serving as initial beta testers. A public release timeline has not yet been announced, but the standalone app is expected to expand access as the beta progresses.
